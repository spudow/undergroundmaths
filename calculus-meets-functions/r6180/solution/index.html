Review question

Can we maximise the volume of this metal tin?

Solution

A cylindrical tin and its tight-fitting cylindrical lid (overlapping the tin by 2cm2cm) are to be made from thin sheet metal. When made, the total area of sheet metal used is 350πcm2350πcm2. The base radius of the tin is xcmxcm and its height is ycmycm as shown in the diagram. Show that, neglecting the thickness of the metal,

x2+xy+2x=175.x2+xy+2x=175.

We know that the surface area of the tin is 350πcm2350πcm2. In terms of xx and yy, the surface area of the tin is:
350π=area of base+area of side+area of side of lid+area of top of lid=πx2+2πxy+2πx×2+πx2=2πx2+2πxy+4πx,350π=area of base+area of side+area of side of lid+area of top of lid=πx2+2πxy+2πx×2+πx2=2πx2+2πxy+4πx,
 and dividing through by 2π2π we get
175=x2+xy+2x.(1)(1)175=x2+xy+2x.

Deduce that the volume, Vcm3Vcm3, of the tin is given by

V=π(175x−2x2−x3).V=π(175x−2x2−x3).

The volume of the tin is

V=πx2y.V=πx2y.

We can eliminate yy by rearranging equation (1)(1) to find that xy=175−x2−2xxy=175−x2−2x, and so

V=πx2y=πx(xy)=πx(175−x2−2x)=π(175x−x3−2x2).V=πx2y=πx(xy)=πx(175−x2−2x)=π(175x−x3−2x2).

If xx may vary, find the values of xx and yy for which VV has its maximum value.

We differentiate VV with respect to xx, giving

dVdx=π(175−3x2−4x)=0,dVdx=π(175−3x2−4x)=0,
so to find stationary points of VV, we must solve
3x2+4x−175=0.3x2+4x−175=0.

We can factorise this equation as (3x+25)(x−7)=0(3x+25)(x−7)=0 (or use the quadratic formula).

Since xx is the radius of the tin, it must be positive, so x=7x=7.

We have that

d2Vdx2=π(−6x−4),d2Vdx2=π(−6x−4),
which is negative when xx is positive, so x=7x=7 gives a local maximum for VV.

Furthermore, as there are no other stationary points for VV with x>0x>0, this must give the maximum possible value for VV for x≥0x≥0.

We need to find the corresponding y.y. We saw in the previous part that

xy=175−x2−2x,xy=175−x2−2x,
so that
7y=175−49−14=112,7y=175−49−14=112,
 and hence y=16y=16.
