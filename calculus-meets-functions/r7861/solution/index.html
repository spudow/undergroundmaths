When does a cubic curve have two turning points?

Solution

  1. Find a condition on the coefficients aa, bb, cc such that the curve has two distinct turning points if, and only if, this condition is satisfied.

The curve has two distinct turning points if and only if the derivative, f′(x)f′(x), has two distinct real roots.

Now
f′(x)=3x2+2ax+b,(1)(1)f′(x)=3x2+2ax+b,

(2a)2−4(3)(b)>0,(2a)2−4(3)(b)>0,

a2−3b>0.a2−3b>0.
