Review question

Can we show that the area of this hexagon is 2(x2+xy+y2)2(x2+xy+y2)?

Solution

The lengths of the two smaller sides of a right-angled triangle are xx in. and yy in. A square is constructed on each side of the triangle and the exterior points of the squares are joined to form a hexagon as in the diagram above. Show that the area of the hexagon is 2(x2+xy+y2)2(x2+xy+y2) sq. in.

The area, AA, of the hexagon is the sum of the seven constituent parts that are labelled in the diagram below. We have also labelled some angles.

We will write AnAn for the area of the nnth region, so we have that
A1=xy2,A2=z2,A4=x2,A5=xy2,A6=y2A1=xy2,A2=z2,A4=x2,A5=xy2,A6=y2

We need to express A2A2 in terms of xx and yy. By Pythagoras theorem, x2+y2=z2x2+y2=z2, so A2=x2+y2A2=x2+y2.

We still need to find A3A3 and A7A7 in terms of xx and yy.
