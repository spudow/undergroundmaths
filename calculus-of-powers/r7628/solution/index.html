Review question

If we take a slice from this sphere, where is its centre of gravity?

Solution

A portion of a solid sphere of radius aa is cut off by means of a plane whose distance from the centre of the sphere is 12a12a. Find the volume of the portion cut off.

Here is a section through the centre of the sphere. With this choice of axes, the equation of the circle is x2+y2=a2x2+y2=a2, and hence y2=a2−x2y2=a2−x2.

The portion cut off is shown in grey. Its volume can be found by revolving the circular arc for a2<x<aa2<x<a about the xx-axis.

Thus the required volume is
∫aa/2πy2dx=π∫aa/2a2−x2dx=π[a2x−x33]aa/2=π(a3−a33−a32+a324)=πa3(24−8−12+124)=524πa3.∫a/2aπy2dx=π∫a/2aa2−x2dx=π[a2x−x33]a/2a=π(a3−a33−a32+a324)=πa3(24−8−12+124)=524πa3.

Find also the distance from the circular plane face of the centre of gravity of this portion.

By symmetry, the centre of gravity (or centre of mass) lies on the xx-axis in our diagram. To find out where on the axis, we take moments about OO and treat the sphere as having density of 11.

We divide the grey solid into discs, each with its centre xx from OO, with thickness δxδx and radius yy. The volume of a disc is πy2δxπy2δx.

The moment of each disc about OO is xπy2δxxπy2δx.

We now let the thickness of the discs tend to zero, at which point the sum of these moments becomes an integral. Let the centre of mass of the grey portion be at a distance x¯x¯ from OO.

x¯524πa3=∫aa/2xπy2dx=π∫aa/2x(a2−x2)dx=π[a2x22−x44]aa/2=π(a42−a44−a48+a464)=π964a4,x¯524πa3=∫a/2axπy2dx=π∫a/2ax(a2−x2)dx=π[a2x22−x44]a/2a=π(a42−a44−a48+a464)=π964a4,
 and therefore
x¯=964πa4524πa3=2740a.x¯=964πa4524πa3=2740a.
 The required distance from the plane is therefore
2740a−12a=740a.2740a−12a=740a.

It is interesting to note that the centre of mass is not the same as the point which has exactly half the mass of the grey solid on each side of it. This latter point, X¯X¯, can be found by integration and is such that X¯≈0.6571aX¯≈0.6571a, whereas the centre of mass, x¯=0.675ax¯=0.675a.

This is equivalent to the distinction between the median and the mean of the distribution of mass.
