Calculus of Powers
Review question

What happens if we expand, then differentiate?
Add to your resource collection

Ref: R8931
Printable/supporting materials Fullscreen mode Teacher notes
  • Question
  • Solution

Solution

The expression

d2dx2[(2x−1)4(1−x)5]+ddx[(2x+1)4(3x2−2)2]d2dx2[(2x−1)4(1−x)5]+ddx[(2x+1)4(3x2−2)2]
is a polynomial of degree

  1. 99;

  2. 88;

  3. 77;

  4. less than 77.

Let’s observe that

  1. multiplying two polynomials of degrees pp and qq gives a polynomial of degree p+qp+q, and

  2. differentiating a polynomial of degree pp gives a polynomial of degree p−1p−1.

From this we can quickly see that the expression in the question is a sum of two polynomials, each of degree 7.

Does this mean their sum has to be a polynomial of degree 7 too?

Yes, unless the leading terms of the two expressions cancel when you add them together.

So what are our leading terms here?

The expression (2x−1)4(1−x)5(2x−1)4(1−x)5 gives first term (2x)4(−x)5=−16x9(2x)4(−x)5=−16x9, which differentiates twice to

−9×8×16x7.−9×8×16x7.

The leading term of the expression (2x+1)4(3x2−2)2(2x+1)4(3x2−2)2 is (2x)4(3x2)2=16×9x8(2x)4(3x2)2=16×9x8, which differentiates to

16×9×8x7.16×9×8x7.

So in fact the two leading terms here DO cancel, and we’re left with a polynomial of degree less than 77, so the answer is (d).
