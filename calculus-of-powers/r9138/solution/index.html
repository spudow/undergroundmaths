Calculus of Powers
Review question

Can we find the area enclosed by this quadratic and the xx-axis?

Solution

The curve y=ax2+bx+cy=ax2+bx+c passes through the points (1,0)(1,0) and (2,0)(2,0) and its gradient at the point (2,0)(2,0) is 22. Find the numerical value of the area included between the curve and the axis of xx.

Since the curve passes through (1,0)(1,0) and (2,0)(2,0) we have
ax2+bx+c=a(x−1)(x−2)=ax2−3ax+2aax2+bx+c=a(x−1)(x−2)=ax2−3ax+2a
 Equating coefficients we find that
band c=−3a=2a(1)(2)(1)b=−3a(2)and c=2a

We also know the gradient of the curve is 22 at x=2x=2. Differentiating,

dydx=2ax+b,dydx=2ax+b,
and so we have that
4a+b=2.4a+b=2.
 Substituting (1)(1) and (2)(2), we have
a=2,b=−6,c=4.a=2,b=−6,c=4.
 So the curve has the equation y=2x2−6x+4y=2x2−6x+4.

The area between the curve and the axis is found by integrating between the two roots at x=1x=1 and x=2x=2. Since the curve is below the axis this will give us a negative result whose numerical value is the area.

∫212x2−6x+4dx=[2x33−3x2+4x]21=163−12+8−23+3−4=143−5=−13∫122x2−6x+4dx=[2x33−3x2+4x]12=163−12+8−23+3−4=143−5=−13
Thus the required area is 1313.
