Review question

When is the volume of this cuboid a maximum?

Solution

A cuboid has a total surface area of 150cm2150cm2 and is such that its base is a square of side xcmxcm.

Show that the height, hcmhcm, of the cuboid is given by h=75−x22xh=75−x22x.

A cuboid with side lengths xx, xx and hh has a surface area of

S=2x2+4xh.S=2x2+4xh.

We know that the total surface area should be 150cm2150cm2, so

150⟹150−2x2⟹h=2x2+4xh=4xh=75−x22x.150=2x2+4xh⟹150−2x2=4xh⟹h=75−x22x.

Express the volume of the cuboid in terms of xx.

A cuboid with side lengths xx, xx and hh has a volume of V=x2hV=x2h. Now

V=x2h=x2(75−x22x)=752x−12x3.V=x2h=x2(75−x22x)=752x−12x3.

Hence determine, as xx varies, its maximum volume and show that this volume is a maximum.

We have VV as a function of xx, so to find the stationary points we differentiate, and set the result equal to zero. We have

V′(x)=752−32x2.V′(x)=752−32x2.

Putting this equal to zero, we see

752−32x2⟹752⟹25⟹x=0=32x2=x2=±5.752−32x2=0⟹752=32x2⟹25=x2⟹x=±5.

Since xx is a length, it must be positive, implying x=5x=5.

Is our solution a maximum? We know V=0.5x(75−x2)V=0.5x(75−x2), and so the equation V=0V=0 has three roots, 00 and ±75⎯⎯⎯⎯√±75. Given the coefficient of x3x3 is negative, the curve must look like this:

Thus x=5x=5 gives us a maximum for VV.

Or we could show x=5x=5 gives a maximum by evaluating the second derivative of VV, giving

V″(x)⟹V″(5)=−3x=−15.V″(x)=−3x⟹V″(5)=−15.

The second derivative is negative at x=5x=5, so the volume has a maximum here, as required.

Now all that remains is to find the volume of the cuboid when x=5x=5, which is given by

V(x)⟹V(5)=752x−12x3=752×5−12×53=3752−1252=2502=125.V(x)=752x−12x3⟹V(5)=752×5−12×53=3752−1252=2502=125.

Therefore the maximum volume of the cuboid is 125cm3125cm3.
