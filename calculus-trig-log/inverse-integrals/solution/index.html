Solution

Use the diagrams to help you find ∫32lnxdx∫23ln⁡xdx.

This solution will be based on ideas from the warm-up, so you may wish to look at the Warm-up ideas section if you haven’t already done so.

The integral represents the area of the shaded region under the curve y=lnxy=ln⁡x. However, we also know that because these graphs are inverse functions, the shaded region between exex and the yy-axis has the same area.

If we don’t know how to integrate lnxln⁡x directly, then we need to use other areas that we do know how to find. We have already calculated the areas of the large rectangle, 3ln33ln⁡3, and the smaller rectangle, 2ln22ln⁡2.

Therefore the L-shaped shaded region has area 3ln3−2ln23ln⁡3−2ln⁡2.

The area between the curve, the yy-axis, ln2ln⁡2 and ln3ln⁡3 is given by the integral
∫ln3ln2eydy=[ey]ln3ln2=3−2=1.∫ln⁡2ln⁡3eydy=[ey]ln⁡2ln⁡3=3−2=1.
 Putting all these together means the area represented by ∫32lnxdx∫23ln⁡xdx can be found by
∫32lnxdx=3ln3−2ln2−∫ln3ln2eydy=3ln3−2ln2−1∫23ln⁡xdx=3ln⁡3−2ln⁡2−∫ln⁡2ln⁡3eydy=3ln⁡3−2ln⁡2−1

The answer can be written in several different forms. If you combined the logarithms you might have ended up with ln(274)−1ln⁡(274)−1 or ln(274e)ln⁡(274e).
