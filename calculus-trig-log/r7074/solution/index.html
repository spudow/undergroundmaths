Where do the curves y=sin2xy=sin⁡2x and y=sinxy=sin⁡x cross?

Solution

The diagram shows part of the graphs of the curves y=sin2xy=sin⁡2x and y=sinxy=sin⁡x.

AA is a maximum point of the curve y=sin2xy=sin⁡2x. BB is a maximum point of the curve y=sinxy=sin⁡x.

CC is the point of intersection of the curves shown. Find

  1. the xx co-ordinate of CC,

To find the xx-coordinate of CC, we need to solve the equation sinx=sin2xsin⁡x=sin⁡2x in the region 0≤x≤12π0≤x≤12π.

Now sinx=sin2x⟺sinx=2sinxcosxsin⁡x=sin⁡2x⟺sin⁡x=2sin⁡xcos⁡x. We can see that sinxsin⁡x is not zero at CC, so the solution we seek is cosx=12cos⁡x=12, and so x=π3x=π3.

  1. the shaded area.

Let’s call the shaded area RR. To find RR, we start with the area of the rectangle spanned by the line ABAB and the xx-axis, which is clearly π4π4.

We then subtract the area below the curves y=sinxy=sin⁡x and y=sin2xy=sin⁡2x as shown by the red area and blue area respectively in the diagram. Thus

R=π4−∫π/3π/4sin2xdx−∫π/2π/3sinxdx.R=π4−∫π/4π/3sin⁡2xdx−∫π/3π/2sin⁡xdx.

Now

∫π/2π/3sinxdx=[−cosx]π/2π/3=12.∫π/3π/2sin⁡xdx=[−cos⁡x]π/3π/2=12.

And thinking about stretching horizontally

∫π/3π/4sin2xdx=12∫2π/3π/2sinxdx=12[−cosx]2π/3π/2=14.∫π/4π/3sin⁡2xdx=12∫π/22π/3sin⁡xdx=12[−cos⁡x]π/22π/3=14.

Thus

R=π4−34.R=π4−34.
