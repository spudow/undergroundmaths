Review question

Can we find the area between sinxsin⁡x and sin2xsin⁡2x?

Solution

Using the same axes, sketch the curves y=sinxy=sin⁡x and y=sin2xy=sin⁡2x from x=0x=0 to 12π12π, where xx is in radians.

The curve y=sin2xy=sin⁡2x has the same shape as y=sinxy=sin⁡x, but twice the frequency; we thus have the following sketch.

Prove that the curves intersect at the points

(0,0)and(π3,3⎯⎯√2).(0,0)and(π3,32).

The curves intersect exactly when sinx=sin2xsin⁡x=sin⁡2x, or sinx=2sinxcosxsin⁡x=2sin⁡xcos⁡x.

This gives us that either sinx=0sin⁡x=0 or cosx=12cos⁡x=12. Within our region this means x=0x=0 or x=π3x=π3.

So the curves intersect at (0,0)(0,0) and at (π3,3⎯⎯√2)(π3,32).

Calculate the area of the region bounded by the parts of the curves between these two points.

The area we are to calculate is the yellow area indicated in the following diagram.
