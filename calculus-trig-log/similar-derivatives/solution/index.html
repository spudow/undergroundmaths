Solution

Can you explain why the points given in the diagram below have coordinates (1,tanθ)(1,tan⁡θ) and (cotθ,1)(cot⁡θ,1)?

Now move the slider for δθδθ.

  • What is the relationship between the blue angle and the red angle?

  • What can you say about the blue triangles and the red triangle as δθδθ decreases to 00?

Download GeoGebra file

You may find it helpful to look at the circles section of Going round in circles to see how tanθtan⁡θ and cotθcot⁡θ relate to the unit circle.

Move the slider so that the blue triangles are visible. Note that the blue angle is θ+δθθ+δθ and the red angle is θθ. Therefore the blue triangles are both similar to the triangle with angle θ+δθθ+δθ and right-angle at (1,0).(1,0). This triangle tends to the red triangle as δθδθ tends to 00 (written δθ→0δθ→0), so the blue triangles get closer and closer to being similar to the red triangle as δθ→0δθ→0.

Consider the blue triangle with one vertex at (1,tanθ)(1,tan⁡θ).

  • What is the length of the circular arc drawn through (1,tanθ)(1,tan⁡θ) and subtended by δθδθ?

In triangle OQPOQP, Pythagoras’ theorem tells us that OP=secθOP=sec⁡θ (note that θθ is acute in this case, so secθ>0sec⁡θ>0).

Alternatively, cosθ=1OPcos⁡θ=1OP so OP=1cosθ=secθOP=1cos⁡θ=sec⁡θ.

OPOP is the radius of the circular arc drawn through PP and subtended by δθδθ, so the length of this arc is secθδθ.sec⁡θδθ. For small δθδθ, we can therefore approximate PSPS by

PS≈secθδθ.PS≈sec⁡θδθ.

Why is it important that θθ and δθδθ are measured in radians?

  • Which side length shows the increase in tanθtan⁡θ as θθ increases by δθδθ? This can be denoted by δ(tanθ)δ(tan⁡θ).

PT=tan(θ+δθ)−tanθPT=tan⁡(θ+δθ)−tan⁡θ, so PTPT shows the increase in tanθtan⁡θ as θθ increases by δθδθ. We’ll write this as δ(tanθ)δ(tan⁡θ) as it is the change in tanθtan⁡θ.

How can you use these ideas to find the derivative of tanθtan⁡θ?

The derivative of tanθtan⁡θ is given by

limδθ→0tan(θ+δθ)−tanθδθlimδθ→0tan⁡(θ+δθ)−tan⁡θδθ
so using our notation, we are interested in δ(tanθ)δθ.δ(tan⁡θ)δθ.

What can the blue and red triangles tell us about this fraction?

We have approximations for the lengths of two sides of the blue triangle when δθδθ is small. As δθ→0δθ→0, the blue and red triangles get closer to being similar, so it may help to consider corresponding sides and angles in the two triangles.


For small δθδθ, the corresponding ratios of sides gives us PTPS≈OPOQPTPS≈OPOQ, so

δ(tanθ)secθδθ≈secθ1or equivalentlyδ(tanθ)δθ≈sec2θ.δ(tan⁡θ)sec⁡θδθ≈sec⁡θ1or equivalentlyδ(tan⁡θ)δθ≈sec2⁡θ.

Taking the limit as δθ→0δθ→0 then gives the derivative of tanθtan⁡θ,

ddθ(tanθ)=sec2θ.ddθ(tan⁡θ)=sec2⁡θ.

What does this say about the sign of the gradient function of tanθtan⁡θ? Can you connect this with the graph of tanθtan⁡θ?

What about the derivative of secθsec⁡θ?

We’ll refer back to our first labelled diagram. The length of the hypotenuse of triangle OQPOQP is secθsec⁡θ, so as θθ increases by δθ,δθ, STST shows the increase in secθ.sec⁡θ.


How can you use these triangles to show that ddθ(secθ)=secθtanθddθ(sec⁡θ)=sec⁡θtan⁡θ?

Can you take a similar approach to find the derivatives of cotθcot⁡θ and cosecθcosec⁡θ? You may be able to do this in more than one way.

The blue triangle with a vertex at (cotθ,1)(cot⁡θ,1) can be used to find derivatives of cotθcot⁡θ and cosecθcosec⁡θ, so we’ll label the vertices of this triangle as well.

You could use a similar approach to before, by considering ratios of sides in

  • triangles UVMUVM and MNOMNO (since MNOMNO is similar to OQPOQP and OM=cosecθOM=cosec⁡θ), or
  • triangles UVMUVM and OQPOQP (since the sides of OQPOQP don’t depend on δθδθ), or
  • triangles UVMUVM and PSTPST (these are similar even if δθδθ isn’t small).

We’ll use triangles UVMUVM and MNOMNO, but what happens if you use different triangles?

We can start in a similar way, noting that MV≈cosecθδθ.MV≈cosec⁡θδθ. One thing we have to be careful about though is that as θθ increases by δθδθ, cotθcot⁡θ and cosecθcosec⁡θ decrease. Therefore we’ll write UM=−δ(cotθ).UM=−δ(cot⁡θ).

We can compare ratios of sides in triangles UVMUVM and MNOMNO to get UMVM≈NM1UMVM≈NM1.

Therefore we have −δ(cotθ)cosecθδθ≈cosecθ1−δ(cot⁡θ)cosec⁡θδθ≈cosec⁡θ1 which leads to ddθ(cotθ)=−cosec2θ.ddθ(cot⁡θ)=−cosec2⁡θ.

By writing UV=−δ(cosecθ)UV=−δ(cosec⁡θ), we can also show that ddθ(cosecθ)=−cosecθcotθ.ddθ(cosec⁡θ)=−cosec⁡θcot⁡θ.

We have worked with a diagram in which θθ is acute. Do these results hold for any value of θθ?
