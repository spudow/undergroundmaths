How does the function change as aa increases? You might have talked about the period of the graph decreasing or, if you thought about it as a transformation, you might describe a stretch parallel to the xx axis. What is the scale factor of the stretch?

Thinking about the tangent to the graph, you should notice that the gradient of the tangent is getting steeper as aa increases. The height of the triangle doesn’t change, but when a=2a=2, the base is half the original, and when a=3a=3 the base is a third of the original. How much steeper is the gradient at these two points than when a=1a=1?

Can you generalise some of your observations? As aa increases, what happens to the xx and yy coordinates? What about the gradient?

Decide which of the following is the gradient function of sin3xsin⁡3x and justify your answer.

  1. cos3xcos⁡3x
  2. 3cosx3cos⁡x
  3. 3cos3x3cos⁡3x

Two possible ways to decide which is the gradient function are explored below.

If we start with two points PP and QQ, which lie on sinxsin⁡x and sinaxsin⁡ax respectively, we can compare QQ to PP as we transform the graph from sinxsin⁡x to sin3xsin⁡3x.

Download GeoGebra file

The applet shows that the gradient of sin3xsin⁡3x at QQ is three times as steep as the gradient of sinxsin⁡x at PP. As cosxcos⁡x is the derivative of sinxsin⁡x and has maximum and minimum values of 11 and −1−1, we can argue that our derivative will have maximum and minimum values of 33 and −3−3. This narrows our choice of gradient function to 3cosx3cos⁡x or 3cos3x.3cos⁡3x.

The graph of sin3xsin⁡3x cycles three times as fast as the graph of sinxsin⁡x. We could think about this as a stretch of sinxsin⁡x, parallel to the xx axis, scale factor 1313. What is the relationship between the xx values at PP and QQ? Does this help us eliminate any of the possible answers?

One way we might write our argument down using mathematical notation is:
ddx(sin3xQ)=3×ddx(sinxP)ddx(sin⁡3xQ)=3×ddx(sin⁡xP)

The gradient function of sin3xsin⁡3x at QQ is three times the gradient function of sinxsin⁡x at PP.

ddx(sin3xQ)=3×cosxPddx(sin⁡3xQ)=3×cos⁡xP

We know the derivative of sinxsin⁡x is cosxcos⁡x, so the derivative of sinxP=cosxPsin⁡xP=cos⁡xP.

ddx(sin3xQ)=3×cos3xQddx(sin⁡3xQ)=3×cos⁡3xQ

The relationship between the xx values of PP and QQ is xP=3xQxP=3xQ.

Therefore the gradient function of sin3xsin⁡3x is 3cos3x3cos⁡3x.

We could make a rough sketch of the gradient function by looking at when the gradient is positive, negative and zero. By marking the points where the gradient is zero, we can see that the gradient function will have the same period as sin3xsin⁡3x, and that the gradient function must be a multiple of cos3xcos⁡3x.

We need to think about what this multiple must be. If we compare the gradients of sin3xsin⁡3x and sinxsin⁡x at (0,0)(0,0), we can see that sin3xsin⁡3x is steeper than sinxsin⁡x at this point. Therefore the gradient of sin3xsin⁡3x at (0,0)(0,0) is greater than 11.

This means that, from the options available, the gradient function of sin3xsin⁡3x must be 3cos3x3cos⁡3x.

Alternatively, we might have estimated the gradient of sin3xsin⁡3x at (0,0)(0,0) by considering the gradient of the chord from (0,0)(0,0) to (0.1,sin0.3)(0.1,sin⁡0.3).
