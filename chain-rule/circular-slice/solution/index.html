Solution

How does your answer to ∫ba1−x2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√dx∫ab1−x2dx relate to the following diagram?

Firstly we might notice that the diagram has an area shaded between lines which could be x=ax=a and x=bx=b, so this could be represented by an integral between aa and bb. The curve appears to be a semicircle with radius 11. A unit circle has equation x2+y2=1x2+y2=1, but if this is rearranged to find yy then we get y=±1−x2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√y=±1−x2. The positive root, gives us a semicircle above the xx-axis as all the yy-values must be positive. Therefore the shaded area is represented by the integral

∫ba1−x2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√dx,∫ab1−x2dx,
which we have already worked out using the substitution x=cosθx=cos⁡θ to get
∫ba1−x2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√dx=12b1−b2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√−12arccosb−12a1−a2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√+12arccosa.∫ab1−x2dx=12b1−b2−12arccos⁡b−12a1−a2+12arccos⁡a.

Finding the area with geometry

As it is part of a semicircle, we can break the area down into more familiar shapes. We can find the area of the triangle and the sector shaded below.

The green triangle has area 12b1−b2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√12b1−b2 and the orange sector has area 12(arccosa−arccosb)12(arccos⁡a−arccos⁡b), (to see why, look at the suggestion section). We can also find the area of the triangle with base aa which is 12a1−a2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√12a1−a2.

The total area is found by adding the orange sector and the green triangle with base bb, then subtracting the triangle with base aa. This gives us

12b1−b2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√+12(arccosa−arccosb)−12a1−a2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√.12b1−b2+12(arccos⁡a−arccos⁡b)−12a1−a2.

The geometric pieces can be thought about in a number of ways. You might have started from the answer of the integral which can be written in the form

∫ba1−x2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√dx=F(b)−F(a),∫ab1−x2dx=F(b)−F(a),
where F(b)=12b1−b2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√−12arccosbF(b)=12b1−b2−12arccos⁡b and F(a)=12a1−a2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√−12arccosa.F(a)=12a1−a2−12arccos⁡a.

Can you see which areas are represented by F(b)F(b) and F(a)F(a)?
