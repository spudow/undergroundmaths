Things you might have noticed

Look at the functions in the table below.

Is there a property or feature that is shared by two functions in a row but not the third? If so, we can say that the third function is the odd one out.

Find a reason why each function in the table could be the odd one out in its row.

y=sinx2y=sin⁡x2 y=lnx2y=ln⁡x2 y=tanx(sec2x−1)y=tan⁡x(sec2⁡x−1)
y=9x2−6x+1y=9x2−6x+1 y=ln3xy=ln⁡3x y=3x−1⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√y=3x−1
y=e5xy=e5x y=1x2+4x+4y=1x2+4x+4 y=ex+4y=ex+4

Here are some reasons why each function is the odd one out. Can you think of others?

y=sinx2y=sin⁡x2

dydx=2xcosx2dydx=2xcos⁡x2

y=lnx2y=ln⁡x2

dydx=2xdydx=2x

y=tanx(sec2x−1)y=tan⁡x(sec2⁡x−1)

dydx=3tan2xsec2xdydx=3tan2⁡xsec2⁡x

If I think about the functions that yy is composed from, I notice that I can use u=x2u=x2 for both sinx2sin⁡x2 and lnx2ln⁡x2, but not for tanx(sec2x−1).tan⁡x(sec2⁡x−1). For this reason, tanx(sec2x−1)tan⁡x(sec2⁡x−1) is the odd one out. Alternatively, I could say it is the odd one out because sinx2sin⁡x2 and lnx2ln⁡x2 are both even functions, but tanx(sec2x−1)tan⁡x(sec2⁡x−1) is odd. I could also say that the derivatives of sinx2sin⁡x2 and lnx2ln⁡x2 can be negative, whereas the derivative of tanx(sec2x−1)tan⁡x(sec2⁡x−1) is never negative.

Continuing to think about the derivatives, I notice that the derivatives of sinx2sin⁡x2 and tanx(sec2x−1)tan⁡x(sec2⁡x−1) are both zero when x=0x=0, so the graphs of these functions both have stationary points when x=0x=0, whereas the graph of lnx2ln⁡x2 does not. Alternatively, the graphs of sinx2sin⁡x2 and tanx(sec2x−1)tan⁡x(sec2⁡x−1) have points if inflection while lnx2ln⁡x2 does not.

Finally, I notice that the graphs of lnx2ln⁡x2 and tan3xtan3⁡x have asymptotes, whereas sinx2sin⁡x2 does not, making sinx2sin⁡x2 the odd one out. I can also see that the range of sinx2sin⁡x2 is [−1,1][−1,1] whereas the range of the other two functions is ℝ.R.

y=9x2−6x+1y=9x2−6x+1

dydx=6(3x−1)dydx=6(3x−1)

y=ln3xy=ln⁡3x

dydx=1xdydx=1x

y=3x−1⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√y=3x−1

dydx=323x−1⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√dydx=323x−1

From the graphs of the functions or by solving y=0y=0, I see that all three graphs meet the xx-axis at 1313, but their behaviour at this point is very different. The derivatives of ln3xln⁡3x and 9x2−6x+19x2−6x+1 are both defined when x=13x=13, but the derivative of 3x−1⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√3x−1 is not, making this the odd one out.

As 9x2−6x+1=(3x−1)29x2−6x+1=(3x−1)2, I can compose both this function and 3x−1⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√3x−1 from u=3x−1,u=3x−1, which makes ln3xln⁡3x the odd one out. The graphs of all three functions involve rescaling a standard function by a factor of 1313 parallel to the xx-axis, but ln3x=lnx+ln3ln⁡3x=ln⁡x+ln⁡3, so this is the only one which can also be thought of as a translation parallel to the yy-axis.

How do the different transformations affect the derivatives of each function?

Thinking about gradients and graphs also gives me a reason why 9x2−6x+19x2−6x+1 is the odd one out: the other two functions have gradients that are always positive but decrease as xx increases, whereas 9x2−6x+19x2−6x+1 has a negative gradient for x<13x<13 and its gradient increases for x>13.x>13.

y=e5xy=e5x

dydx=5e5xdydx=5e5x

y=1x2+4x+4y=1x2+4x+4

dydx=−2(x+2)3dydx=−2(x+2)3

y=ex+4y=ex+4

dydx=ex+4dydx=ex+4

Looking at the gradient functions and graphs, I notice that the gradients of y=e5xy=e5x and y=ex+4y=ex+4 are always positive, whereas y=1x2+4x+4y=1x2+4x+4 has negative gradient for x>−2.x>−2. If instead I think about composition of functions, I can write e5x=(ex)5e5x=(ex)5 and ex+4=e4×exex+4=e4×ex, so these are both compositions of exex with another function, making 1x2+4x+41x2+4x+4 the odd one out again.

I notice that ex+4ex+4 is the only function in the row (and the whole table) whose derivative is equal to itself. Thinking again about composition of functions, if I view 1x2+4x+41x2+4x+4 as (x+2)−2(x+2)−2 then this and e5xe5x are both powers of a function, whereas ex+4ex+4 is not.

Which functions could you have identified as an odd one out from the formula? When did the graph help? How much extra information did finding dydxdydx give you?

What features or properties does yy inherit from the functions it’s composed from?

Can you also find an odd one out within each column?

Some of the ideas above can also explain why a function is the odd one out in its column, but there are other reasons too.

y=sinx2y=sin⁡x2

dydx=2xcosx2dydx=2xcos⁡x2

I could say that sinx2sin⁡x2 is the odd one out because the other two functions are standard functions scaled by a constant factor — the derivative of 9x2−6x+19x2−6x+1 has a scale factor of 33 and the derivative of e5xe5x has a scale factor of 5.5.

How do these factors of 33 or 55 arise when we find dydxdydx?

y=9x2−6x+1y=9x2−6x+1

dydx=6(3x−2)dydx=6(3x−2)

When I look at how the gradients of the functions behave, I notice that the second derivatives of e5xe5x and sinx2sin⁡x2 must both depend on xx, whereas the second derivative of 9x2−6x+19x2−6x+1 is constant. This makes 9x2−6x+19x2−6x+1 the odd one out.

y=e5xy=e5x

dydx=5e5xdydx=5e5x

One reason why e5xe5x could be the odd one out is that it is the only function in the column whose graph does not have a line of symmetry.

y=lnx2y=ln⁡x2

dydx=2xdydx=2x

I notice that y=lnx2y=ln⁡x2 is an even function, which could make it the odd one out in the column. It is also the odd one out because the other two functions are translations of standard functions parallel to the axes.

y=ln3xy=ln⁡3x

dydx=1xdydx=1x

Thinking again about lines of symmetry makes y=ln3xy=ln⁡3x the odd one out. Alternatively, it is the only function in this column that only has positive gradient.

y=1x2+4x+4y=1x2+4x+4

dydx=−2(x+2)3dydx=−2(x+2)3

The derivatives of lnx2ln⁡x2 and ln3xln⁡3x both have denominator xx, which distinguishes them from 1x2+4x+4.1x2+4x+4. It is tempting to say that the derivatives of lnx2ln⁡x2 and ln3xln⁡3x are both odd functions, but ln3xln⁡3x is only defined for x>0.x>0.

y=tanx(sec2x−1)y=tan⁡x(sec2⁡x−1)

dydx=3tan2xsec2xdydx=3tan2⁡xsec2⁡x

Either by looking at the derivatives or the graphs, I can see that tanx(sec2x−1)tan⁡x(sec2⁡x−1) is the only function whose graph has stationary points. These are also points of inflection, and neither of the other two functions have points of inflection. In other words, tanx(sec2x−1)tan⁡x(sec2⁡x−1) is the only function whose second derivative changes sign. Note that I don’t have to compute the second derivatives to see this: the graph of ex+4ex+4 is always turning to the left and the graph of 3x−1⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√3x−1 is always turning to the right, whereas each section of the graph of tanx(sec2x−1)tan⁡x(sec2⁡x−1) changes from turning to the right to turning to the left. This idea is discussed in more detail in Gradients of gradients.

y=3x−1⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√y=3x−1

dydx=323x−1⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√dydx=323x−1

The behaviour of the gradients of the graphs also give a reason why 3x−1⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√3x−1 is the odd one out: it is the only function in the column whose graph gets less steep as y→∞.y→∞.

y=ex+4y=ex+4

dydx=ex+4dydx=ex+4

Finally, y=ex+4y=ex+4 is the only function in this column for which the xx-axis is an asymptote. We can see that as x→−∞x→−∞ the value of the function tends to zero.
