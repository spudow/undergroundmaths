Warm-up

A curve in the x−yx−y plane is defined parametrically in terms of a parameter tt as

x=2cost,y=3sint.x=2cos⁡t,y=3sin⁡t.

Find an expression for dydxdydx in terms of tt.

x=2cost⟹dxdty=3sint⟹dydt=−2sint=3costx=2cos⁡t⟹dxdt=−2sin⁡ty=3sin⁡t⟹dydt=3cos⁡t

Using the chain rule,

dydx=dy/dtdx/dt=−3cost2sint.dydx=dy/dtdx/dt=−3cos⁡t2sin⁡t.

Hence find the stationary points on the curve.

Are there any values of tt for which dydxdydx is undefined? What would that mean?

At the stationary points, dydx=0dydx=0 which means
−3cost2sint=0⟹cost=0.−3cos⁡t2sin⁡t=0⟹cos⁡t=0.

We don’t need to know the values of tt; to find xx and yy it is sufficient to know costcos⁡t and sintsin⁡t. When cost=0cos⁡t=0, sint=1sin⁡t=1 or −1−1, so substituting we find the stationary points are at

(0,3) and (0,−3).(0,3) and (0,−3).

dydxdydx is undefined when sint=0sin⁡t=0 which happens at

(−2,0) and (2,0).(−2,0) and (2,0).
A sketch of the curve should make it clear what is happening here.
