Review question

Where is the centre of mass of half this cylinder?

Solution

A uniform solid right circular cylinder of radius aa and height 2a2a stands on a horizontal plane. Rectangular co-ordinate axes OxOx, OyOy are taken in the plane of the base, with OO at the centre of the base. The cylinder is cut into two equal parts by the plane which passes through the line x=−ax=−a and makes an angle of 45∘45∘ with the horizontal. Justify the formula
∫a−a2(x+a)a2−x2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√dx∫−aa2(x+a)a2−x2dx

for the volume of the lower half…

The volume of the lower half can be thought of as the sum of the volumes of thin vertical slices parallel to the yy-axis.

Each slice has a shape approximating a cuboid, with thickness δxδx.

To find the height of a slice we look at the front view of the cylinder. The slice at distance xx from the origin has height z=x+az=x+a.

For the width of a slice, we look at the plan view. The base is a circle of radius aa and Pythagoras’ theorem tells us that x2+y2=a2x2+y2=a2. The width of the slice is twice the yy value, namely 2a2−x2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√2a2−x2.

The volume of each slice, then, is
2a2−x2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√(x+a)δx,2a2−x2(x+a)δx,
 and so the total volume of the bottom half (as in the limit, the sum becomes an integral) is
∫a−a2a2−x2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√(x+a)dx,∫−aa2a2−x2(x+a)dx,

as required.

…and verify by calculation of the integral that the volume is πa3πa3.

Take x=asinθx=asin⁡θ, so that dx=acosθdθdx=acos⁡θdθ. Then
V=∫a−a2a2−x2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√(x+a)dx=∫π/2−π/22a2−a2sin2θ⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√(asinθ+a)acosθdθ=2a3∫π/2−π/2cosθ(sinθ+1)cosθdθ=2a3∫π/2−π/2cos2θsinθ+cos2θdθ.V=∫−aa2a2−x2(x+a)dx=∫−π/2π/22a2−a2sin2⁡θ(asin⁡θ+a)acos⁡θdθ=2a3∫−π/2π/2cos⁡θ(sin⁡θ+1)cos⁡θdθ=2a3∫−π/2π/2cos2⁡θsin⁡θ+cos2⁡θdθ.
 The first term can be integrated by substituting u=cosθu=cos⁡θ, du=−sinθdθdu=−sin⁡θdθ. The second term can be expanded using a trig identity.
V=2a3{∫00−u2du+∫π/2−π/2cos2θ+12dθ}=2a3{0+[sin2θ4+θ2]π/2−π/2}=2a3{0+0+π2}=πa3V=2a3{∫00−u2du+∫−π/2π/2cos⁡2θ+12dθ}=2a3{0+[sin⁡2θ4+θ2]−π/2π/2}=2a3{0+0+π2}=πa3

Prove that the centre of mass of this lower half is vertically above the point (14a,0)(14a,0).

Since the object has reflective symmetry in the x−zx−z plane, the centre of mass must lie in this plane, i.e. the plane y=0y=0.

The xx-coordinate, x0x0, of the centre of mass must be such that the mass would balance around it. Taking moments we have that
∫a−ax×2a2−x2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√(x+a)dx=πa3×x0.∫−aax×2a2−x2(x+a)dx=πa3×x0.

To find this integral we’ll use the substitution x=asinθx=asin⁡θ again and trig identities for sin2θsin⁡2θ and cos2θcos⁡2θ. We have

∫a−ax×2a2−x2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√(x+a)dx=∫π/2−π/2asinθ×2a2−a2sin2θ⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√(asinθ+a)acosθdθ=2a4∫π/2−π/2sinθcosθ(sinθ+1)cosθdθ=2a4∫π/2−π/2cos2θsin2θ+cos2θsinθdθ=2a4∫π/2−π/2sin22θ4+cos2θsinθdθ=2a4∫π/2−π/21−cos4θ8+cos2θsinθdθ=2a4[θ8−sin4θ32−cos3θ3]π/2−π/2=πa44.∫−aax×2a2−x2(x+a)dx=∫−π/2π/2asin⁡θ×2a2−a2sin2⁡θ(asin⁡θ+a)acos⁡θdθ=2a4∫−π/2π/2sin⁡θcos⁡θ(sin⁡θ+1)cos⁡θdθ=2a4∫−π/2π/2cos2⁡θsin2⁡θ+cos2⁡θsin⁡θdθ=2a4∫−π/2π/2sin2⁡2θ4+cos2⁡θsin⁡θdθ=2a4∫−π/2π/21−cos⁡4θ8+cos2⁡θsin⁡θdθ=2a4[θ8−sin⁡4θ32−cos3⁡θ3]−π/2π/2=πa44.

Thus πa44=πa3x0⟹x0=a4,πa44=πa3x0⟹x0=a4, as required, and so the centre of mass of the lower half lies directly above the point (14a,0)(14a,0).
