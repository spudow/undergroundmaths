  1. How could you work out the gradient of y=1x+1y=1x+1 at the point (2,13)(2,13)?

  2. How can you differentiate 1x+11x+1?

There are several ways to find the gradient function or derivative of 1x+1.1x+1. The approaches we discuss here assume that you do not already know how to differentiate this function directly.

The graph of y=1x+1y=1x+1 is a translation y=1x.y=1x.

Therefore the tangent to y=1x+1y=1x+1 at (2,13)(2,13) is parallel to the tangent to y=1xy=1x at (3,13).(3,13).

It follows that the gradient of y=1x+1y=1x+1 where x=2x=2 is the same as the gradient of 1x1x where x=3.x=3.

Therefore the gradient of y=1x+1y=1x+1 at (2,13)(2,13) is −19.−19.

Now consider a general point (x,y)(x,y) on the graph of y=1x+1.y=1x+1.

From the ideas in part 1, the gradient of y=1x+1y=1x+1 at a general point (x,y)(x,y) is the same as the gradient of y=1xy=1x at the point with coordinates (x+1,y)(x+1,y).

You can see this by moving the point A in the GeoGebra file above.

Therefore the derivative of 1x+11x+1 is −1(x+1)2.−1(x+1)2.

What would happen if the function we wanted to differentiate had been a translation of y=1xy=1x parallel to the yy-axis instead?

  1. How could you adapt these approaches to work out the derivative of 12x+112x+1?

We already know how to differentiate constant multiples of powers of xx, so we know how to differentiate 12x12x. From the first part of the problem we also know a way of finding the derivatives of horizontal translations of functions. What is y=12x+1y=12x+1 as a translation of y=12xy=12x?

The vertical asymptotes of the two graphs suggest that y=12x+1y=12x+1 is a translation of y=12xy=12x by 1212 in the negative xx-direction.

Function notation can help to show this too.

If we write 12x=f(x)12x=f(x) then 12x+1=12(x+12)=f(x+12).12x+1=12(x+12)=f(x+12).

This means that the gradient of y=12x+1y=12x+1 at (x,y)(x,y) is the same as the gradient of y=12xy=12x at (x+12,y)(x+12,y).

You can see this by moving the point A in the GeoGebra file below. Be careful when the xx-coordinate of A gets close to −12−12.

Download GeoGebra file

It follows that the gradient of y=12x+1y=12x+1 at (x,y)(x,y) is −12(x+12)2.−12(x+12)2.

We can write this as −2(2x+1)2−2(2x+1)2 to help see the relationship between the original function and its derivative.

You could also think of y=12x+1y=12x+1 as a transformation of y=1x.y=1x. How would this affect your approach?
