Circles
Review question

Can we show that these two circles touch?

Solution

Show that the circles having equations x2+y2=25x2+y2=25 and x2+y2−24x−18y+125=0x2+y2−24x−18y+125=0 touch each other. Calculate the coordinates of the point at which they touch.

Approach 1: Geometric

The circle with equation x2+y2=25x2+y2=25 has its centre at the origin and radius 55.

We can complete the square for the equation x2+y2−24x−18y+125=0x2+y2−24x−18y+125=0 to find its centre and radius:

x2+y2−24x−18y+125=0⟹(x−12)2−122+(y−9)2−92+125=0⟹(x−12)2+(y−9)2=144+81−125⟹(x−12)2+(y−9)2=100x2+y2−24x−18y+125=0⟹(x−12)2−122+(y−9)2−92+125=0⟹(x−12)2+(y−9)2=144+81−125⟹(x−12)2+(y−9)2=100
so this circle has centre (12,9)(12,9) and radius 1010.

We can sketch these:

It seems as though the circles touch. Let’s prove that!

The length of the line joining the two centres (0,0)(0,0) and (12,9)(12,9) is (using Pythagoras)

122+92⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√=144+81⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√=225⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√=15.122+92=144+81=225=15.

Alternatively, we can notice that 99 and 1212 form the two short sides of right-angled triangle, which must be a 33-44-55 triangle scaled by a factor of 33, and therefore the diagonal length is 3×5=153×5=15.

Now the radii of the two circles are 55 and 1010. Since 5+10=155+10=15 (the distance between the centres), the two circles touch.

To find the coordinates of the point where they touch, we can use similar triangles:

The small triangle has sides in the ratio a:b:5a:b:5 (base to height to hypotenuse), while in the large triangle, they are in the ratio 12:9:1512:9:15. These ratios are the same, so a:b:5=4:3:5a:b:5=4:3:5, and the point of touching has coordinates (4,3)(4,3).

Another way to think about this is that the point of touching is 515=13515=13 of the way along the line from (0,0)(0,0) to (12,9)(12,9), so it is at (4,3)(4,3).

Approach 2: Algebraic

For the circles to touch, we must show that they intersect at exactly one point.

We have the equations of the two circles:

x2+y2=25x2+y2=25
and
x2+y2−24x−18y+125=0.x2+y2−24x−18y+125=0.

Substituting the first equation into the second (or alternatively subtracting the first equation from the second) gives
⟹⟹25−24x−18y+125=024x+18y=1504x+3y=25.25−24x−18y+125=0⟹24x+18y=150⟹4x+3y=25.

So if the two circles meet in two points, they must both lie on 4x+3y=25.4x+3y=25.

We can rearrange 4x+3y=254x+3y=25 to get
x=14(25−3y)x=14(25−3y)
 and substitute into the equation x2+y2=25x2+y2=25:
⟺⟺⟺⟺(14(25−3y))2+y2=2525y2−150y+225=0y2−6y+9=0(y−3)2=0y=3.(14(25−3y))2+y2=25⟺25y2−150y+225=0⟺y2−6y+9=0⟺(y−3)2=0⟺y=3.

The quadratic equation has a repeated root, so has exactly one solution.

This means that the line 4x+3y=254x+3y=25 is a tangent to the circle x2+y2=25.x2+y2=25.

We can find the corresponding xx value by setting y=3y=3 in the equation 4x+3y=254x+3y=25:
⟹4x+3(3)=25x=4.4x+3(3)=25⟹x=4.
 Substituting
x=14(25−3y)x=14(25−3y)
 into the equation x2+y2−24x−18y+125=0,x2+y2−24x−18y+125=0, we have
⟺⟺⟺⟺(14(25−3y))2+y2−24(14(25−3y))−18y+125=025y2−150y+225=0y2−6y+9=0(y−3)2=0y=3.(14(25−3y))2+y2−24(14(25−3y))−18y+125=0⟺25y2−150y+225=0⟺y2−6y+9=0⟺(y−3)2=0⟺y=3.

This means that the line 4x+3y=254x+3y=25 is a tangent to the second circle also at (4,3)(4,3).

So the circles x2+y2=25x2+y2=25 and x2+y2−24x−18y+125=0x2+y2−24x−18y+125=0 touch at the point (4,3)(4,3).
