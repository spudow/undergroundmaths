Combining Functions
Review question

Which transformations give us the graph of y=2x2−4x+3y=2x2−4x+3?

Suggestion

The graph of the function

y=2x2−4x+3y=2x2−4x+3
can be obtained from the graph of y=2x2y=2x2 by

  1. a stretch parallel to the yy-axis followed by a translation parallel to the yy-axis.
  2. a translation parallel to the xx-axis followed by a stretch parallel to the yy-axis.
  3. a translation parallel to the xx-axis followed by one parallel to the xx-axis.
  4. a translation parallel to the xx-axis followed by a reflection in the yy-axis.
  5. a reflection parallel to the yy-axis followed by a translation parallel to the yy-axis.

The starting and finishing functions differ by x2↦x2−4x+3x2↦x2−4x+3. Could we rewrite the latter to look like a transformation?
