Review question

Can we find the equation of the plane containing l1l1 and PQPQ?

Solution

The lines l1l1 and l2l2 have equations r=a+sbr=a+sb and r=c+tdr=c+td, where ss and tt are real numbers and

a=5i+6j−k,b=i+j+k,c=i+13j+7k,d=i+6j+3k.a=5i+6j−k,b=i+j+k,c=i+13j+7k,d=i+6j+3k.

Also, the point PP on l1l1 and the point QQ on l2l2 are such that PQPQ is perpendicular to both l1l1 and l2.l2. In any order:

  1. show that PQ=38⎯⎯⎯⎯√,PQ=38,

  2. find the position vectors of PP and Q,Q,

We can write the position vector of PP as p=5+s6+s−1+sp=(5+s6+s−1+s), and the position vector of QQ as q=1+t13+6t7+3t.q=(1+t13+6t7+3t).

Thus q−p=t−s−46t−s+73t−s+8q−p=(t−s−46t−s+73t−s+8). This vector has to be perpendicular to both l1l1 and l2l2, and so using the scalar product,

t−s−46t−s+73t−s+8.111=0andt−s−46t−s+73t−s+8.163=0.(t−s−46t−s+73t−s+8).(111)=0and(t−s−46t−s+73t−s+8).(163)=0.

This gives us the two equations 10t−3s+11=010t−3s+11=0 and 46t−10s+62=046t−10s+62=0, which solve to give s=−3s=−3, t=−2t=−2.

Thus p=23−4p=(23−4) and q=−111q=(−111). We also have q−p=−3−25q−p=(−3−25), and so ||q−p||=(−3)2+(−2)2+52⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√=38⎯⎯⎯⎯√|q−p|=(−3)2+(−2)2+52=38.

  1. obtain the Cartesian equation of the plane containing l1l1 and PQPQ.

Using vector product we can find a vector perpendicular to two others, thus

(q−p)×b=n(q−p)×b=n
where nn is a vector perpendicular to both bb (the direction vector of l1l1) and PQPQ.

−3−25×111=−2−5−(−3−5)−3−−2=−78−1(−3−25)×(111)=(−2−5−(−3−5)−3−−2)=(−78−1)

A normal vector immediately yields the vector equation of the plane r.n=dr.n=d where dd can be calculated from any known point on the plane.

So q.n=7+8−1=d=14q.n=7+8−1=d=14.

Hence the vector equation of the plane is

xyz.−78−1=14(xyz).(−78−1)=14
which gives rise to the Cartesian equation −7x+8y−z=14−7x+8y−z=14 or
7x−8y+z+14=07x−8y+z+14=0

Check; is aa on this plane? Substituting in the coordinates we have 7(5)−8(6)+(−1)+14=07(5)−8(6)+(−1)+14=0, so yes.

As an alternative method, an equation of the plane containing both PQPQ and l1l1 is

r=xyz=23−4+λ111+μ32−5.r=(xyz)=(23−4)+λ(111)+μ(32−5).

Thus x=2+λ+3μx=2+λ+3μ, y=3+λ+2μy=3+λ+2μ, z=−4+λ−5μz=−4+λ−5μ.

Subtracting the first two, x−y=−1+μx−y=−1+μ, and so μ=x−y+1μ=x−y+1.

Subtracting the second two, y−z=7+7μy−z=7+7μ, and so y−z−7=7(x−y+1)y−z−7=7(x−y+1).

This becomes 7x−8y+z+14=07x−8y+z+14=0, which is the equation of the plane we seek.
