Combining Vectors
Review question

Given three vectors, can we find a point equidistant from them all?

Solution

The position vectors of points AA, BB and CC, referred to an origin OO, are aa, bb and cc respectively, where

abc=3i+4j+5k,=7i−k,=5i+5j.a=3i+4j+5k,b=7i−k,c=5i+5j.

PP is a point which is equidistant from the lines OAOA, OBOB and OCOC. Write down expressions for the cosine of the angle between OPOP and aa, bb and cc respectively and hence deduce an expression for a unit vector in the direction of OPOP.

Let A′A′ be a point on OAOA such that A′PA′P is perpendicular to OAOA and let B′B′ and C′C′ be the equivalent points on OBOB and OCOC respectively.

If we think about the three right angled triangles A′OPA′OP, B′OPB′OP and C′OPC′OP we have hypotenuse OPOP common to each and PP was defined to be equidistant from the lines OAOA, OBOB and OCOC. So OA′=OB′=OC′OA′=OB′=OC′ making the triangles congruent (RHS).

Given that the line OPOP meets the plane ABCABC at QQ, find OQOQ.

We can define the plane ABCABC from the three position vectors aa, bb and cc via

r=a+λ(b−a)+μ(c−a),r=a+λ(b−a)+μ(c−a),
where λλ and μμ are scalar parameters.

If we start at the origin, aa takes us onto the plane. The vectors (b−a)(b−a) and (c−a)(c−a) are distinct vectors lying in the plane, so a linear combination of these will take us anywhere on the plane from aa.

Thus the plane ABCABC is

r=(3+4λ+2μ)i+(4−4λ+μ)j+(5−6λ−5μ)k.r=(3+4λ+2μ)i+(4−4λ+μ)j+(5−6λ−5μ)k.

The line OPOP is given by r=t(13i+4j+6k)r=t(13i+4j+6k). To find the intersection we set these equal, then the three components give us three equations in three unknowns, λλ, μμ and tt.

Solving these simultaneously gives t=513t=513 and so OQOQ = 5i+2013j+3013k5i+2013j+3013k.

Given that GG is the centroid of the triangle ABCABC, show that QGQG is parallel to the coordinate plane OyzOyz.

The position vector of the centroid GG of a triangle whose vertices have position vectors aa, bb and cc is given by

g=a+b+c3.g=a+b+c3.
In our case
g=5i+3j+43k.g=5i+3j+43k.

The direction of the line QGQG is given by the vector g−OQg−OQ.

If this direction vector is parallel to the OyzOyz plane, its xx-component will be zero. Hence we have to show that qi=giqi=gi.

The xx-components of gg and OQOQ are both 55, so QGQG is indeed parallel to the plane.
