Combining Vectors
Review question

If OM−→−OM→ is the projection of OA−→−OA→ on mm, where is MM?

Solution

The straight lines ll and mm intersect at the origin OO, and their directions are parallel to ii and i+2ji+2j respectively. The point AA has position vector OA−→−OA→ given by 2i+3j+5k2i+3j+5k. The point LL on ll is such that OL−→OL→ is the projection of OA−→−OA→ on ll, and the point MM on mm is such that OM−→−OM→ is the projection of OA−→−OA→ on mm. State the position vector of LL, and show that OM−→−=85i+165jOM→=85i+165j.

Since ll is parallel to ii (or the xx-axis), the vector OL−→OL→ is the ii-component of OA−→−OA→. So the position vector of LL is 2i2i.

The point MM is on mm, so its position vector can be written as μ(i+2j)μ(i+2j) for some number μμ. Since it is the projection, it follows that mm and AM−→−AM→ are perpendicular. In other words, their dot product is zero.
(i+2j).((μ−2)i+(2μ−3)j−5k)⟹(μ−2)+2(2μ−3)⟹μ=0=0=85(i+2j).((μ−2)i+(2μ−3)j−5k)=0⟹(μ−2)+2(2μ−3)=0⟹μ=85

So OM−→−=85i+165jOM→=85i+165j as required.
