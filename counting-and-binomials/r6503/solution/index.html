Counting & Binomials
Review question

Can we estimate a difference of eighth powers?
Add to your resource collection

Ref: R6503

Solution

Write down the first four terms in the expansion of (1+x)8(1+x)8 in ascending powers of xx.

We can use the binomial theorem for nn a positive integer,
(a+b)n=∑k=0n(nk)an−kbk,(a+b)n=∑k=0n(nk)an−kbk,
 with n=8n=8, a=1a=1 and b=xb=x, to get
(1+x)8=(80)x0+(81)x1+(82)x2+(83)x3+⋯+x8=1+81x0+8⋅72⋅1x2+8⋅7⋅63⋅2⋅1x3+⋯x8=1+8x+28x2+56x3+⋯+x8.(1+x)8=(80)x0+(81)x1+(82)x2+(83)x3+⋯+x8=1+81x0+8⋅72⋅1x2+8⋅7⋅63⋅2⋅1x3+⋯x8=1+8x+28x2+56x3+⋯+x8.

Alternatively, we can just use the fact that the 9th line of Pascal’s triangle starts 1, 8, 28, 56, … and just write the final line down.

Evaluate (1.001)8−(0.999)8(1.001)8−(0.999)8 to eight significant figures.

We can expand to get

(1−x)8=1−8x+28x2−56x3+⋯+x8.(1−x)8=1−8x+28x2−56x3+⋯+x8.
Thus
(1+x)8−(1−x)8=16x+112x3+112x5+16x7,(1+x)8−(1−x)8=16x+112x3+112x5+16x7,
 with the even powers of xx all cancelling out.

If we pick x=0.001x=0.001 and substitute it into the above equation we get precisely what we need:
(1.001)8−(0.999)8≈16×0.001+112×(0.001)3=0.016+0.000000112=0.016000112.(1)(2)(1)(1.001)8−(0.999)8≈16×0.001+112×(0.001)3(2)=0.016+0.000000112=0.016000112.

So to eight significant figures (1.001)8−(0.999)8≈0.01600011(1.001)8−(0.999)8≈0.01600011.

Do the higher-order terms make a contribution that we need to consider?

The x5x5 term does not contribute because (0.001)5=0.000000000000001(0.001)5=0.000000000000001 and the coefficient is not of the order 106106 that would be needed to bring it within 8 significant figures. The other terms are even smaller.
