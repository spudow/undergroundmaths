Counting & Binomials
Review question

Can we find an approximation to 5⎯⎯√+3⎯⎯√5+3?
Add to your resource collection

Ref: R7477
Printable/supporting materials Fullscreen mode Teacher notes
  • Question
  • Solution

Solution

If baba is small enough for powers of baba higher than the third to be neglected, show that
(a+b)12−(a−b)12=a12(ba+b38a3).(a+b)12−(a−b)12=a12(ba+b38a3).

If a>0a>0, we can write

(a+b)12=a⎯⎯√(1+ba)1/2.(a+b)12=a(1+ba)1/2.

The binomial theorem says that, if ||x||<1|x|<1,
(1+x)α=1+αx+α(α−1)2!x2+α(α−1)(α−2)3!x3+⋯(1+x)α=1+αx+α(α−1)2!x2+α(α−1)(α−2)3!x3+⋯
 and so, if baba is small enough,
(a+b)12=a⎯⎯√(1+ba)1/2=a⎯⎯√(1+12ba+12×(−12)2!(ba)2+12×(−12)×(−32)3!(ba)3+⋯)≈a⎯⎯√(1+b2a−b28a2+b316a3).(a+b)12=a(1+ba)1/2=a(1+12ba+12×(−12)2!(ba)2+12×(−12)×(−32)3!(ba)3+⋯)≈a(1+b2a−b28a2+b316a3).
 Similarly, assuming that a>0a>0 and baba is small enough we will get the same result as above replacing bb with −b−b,
(a−b)12≈a⎯⎯√(1+−b2a−(−b)28a2+(−b)316a3)=a⎯⎯√(1−b2a−b28a2−b316a3).(a−b)12≈a(1+−b2a−(−b)28a2+(−b)316a3)=a(1−b2a−b28a2−b316a3).
 As a result,
(a+b)12−(a−b)12≈a⎯⎯√((1+b2a−b28a2+b316a3)−(1−b2a−b28a2−b316a3))=a⎯⎯√(ba+b38a3).(a+b)12−(a−b)12≈a((1+b2a−b28a2+b316a3)−(1−b2a−b28a2−b316a3))=a(ba+b38a3).

Use this result to deduce a rational approximation to 5⎯⎯√−3⎯⎯√5−3.

Let’s put a+b=5a+b=5 and a−b=3a−b=3. Now adding gives 2a=8⟹a=4,b=12a=8⟹a=4,b=1. Thus a>0a>0 and baba is suitably small.

From our work above,
5⎯⎯√−3⎯⎯√=(a+b)12−(a−b)12≈a⎯⎯√(ba+b38a3)=4⎯⎯√(14+18×64)=12+1256=129256≈0.5039...5−3=(a+b)12−(a−b)12≈a(ba+b38a3)=4(14+18×64)=12+1256=129256≈0.5039...

The approximation is an accurate one: 5⎯⎯√−3⎯⎯√=0.5040...5−3=0.5040...

Deduce, or obtain otherwise, a rational approximation to 5⎯⎯√+3⎯⎯√5+3.

We have (5⎯⎯√+3⎯⎯√)(5⎯⎯√−3⎯⎯√)=2.(5+3)(5−3)=2. Thus 5⎯⎯√+3⎯⎯√≈2×256129=512129=3.9690...5+3≈2×256129=512129=3.9690...

Again, the approximation is a good one: 5⎯⎯√+3⎯⎯√=3.9681...5+3=3.9681...
