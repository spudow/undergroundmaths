Energy solution

The magnitude scale is based on horizontal displacement,

ML=log10(AA0), where A0=1μm.ML=log10⁡(AA0), where A0=1μm.

But the destructive power of an earthquake is better indicated by the amount of energy dissipated. It has been suggested that the energy, EE, is related to the displacement, AA, by a simple power law,

E=E0(AA0)pE=E0(AA0)p
where E0E0 and pp are constants.

Can you use the following data from the British Geological Survey and the US Geological Survey to verify this suggestion and find values for E0E0 and pp?

Earthquake Magnitude, MLML Energy, E(GJ)E(GJ)
San Francisco, USA, 1906 7.87.8 6×1076×107
Chile, 1960 9.59.5 1×10101×1010
Kobe, Japan, 1995 6.86.8 1×1061×106
Kent, 2007 4.34.3 200200
Lincolnshire, 2008 5.25.2 30003000
The given data shows how EE changes with MLML, so first we need to use the formulae above to find a relationship between these two. Using logarithms on the suggested power law, then combining with the definition of MLML,
log10E=log10[E0(AA0)p]=log10E0+plog10(AA0)=log10E0+pML.log10⁡E=log10⁡[E0(AA0)p]=log10⁡E0+plog10⁡(AA0)=log10⁡E0+pML.

If we plot a graph of log10Elog10⁡E against MLML, this should give a straight line with gradient pp and intercept log10E0log10⁡E0.

Indeed the points do lie roughly on a straight line, so we can confirm the suggested relationship. Reading the gradient and intercept from the graph, we find
plog10E0E0=1.5=−4.2=10−4.2≈6.3×10−5GJ=63kJ.p=1.5log10⁡E0=−4.2E0=10−4.2≈6.3×10−5GJ=63kJ.

This value of pp agrees with Charles Richter’s empirical model that states E∝A32E∝A32. A consequence of this is that an increase of 22 in magnitude corresponds to an increase of energy by a factor of 10001000.

Estimate the energy dissipation of the 2011 New Zealand earthquake, magnitude 6.16.1.

This can either be read from the graph, or calculated using the formula which can now be rewritten as
E=E0×103ML2=63×103×6.12≈8.9×1010kJ=8.9×104GJ.E=E0×103ML2=63×103×6.12≈8.9×1010kJ=8.9×104GJ.

This is a little more than the energy released by the atomic bomb that was dropped on Hiroshima in 1945.
