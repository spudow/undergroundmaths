Energy

The magnitude scale is based on horizontal displacement,

ML=log10(AA0), where A0=1μm.ML=log10⁡(AA0), where A0=1μm.

But the destructive power of an earthquake is better indicated by the amount of energy dissipated. It has been suggested that the energy, EE, is related to the displacement, AA, by a simple power law,

E=E0(AA0)pE=E0(AA0)p
where E0E0 and pp are constants.

Can you use the following data from the British Geological Survey and the US Geological Survey to verify this suggestion and find values for E0E0 and pp?
