Problem

The following data is an extract from a planetary fact sheet published by NASA.

Physics suggests that the orbital period, PP, and distance from the sun, rr, are related by a formula of the form

P=ArkP=Ark
where AA and kk are constants. By plotting a suitable graph, estimate the values of AA and kk.

  • A straight-forward graph of PP against rr will probably show a curve from which it is hard to estimate values.
  • Can you transform the data to give a straight line graph?
  • You will probably find it helpful to use either graph paper or graphing software to obtain accurate values.

Use your results to predict the orbital period of Ceres, a dwarf planet in the asteroid belt with r=414.0r=414.0.

The surface temperature is generally lower for planets further away from the sun. Use the data to find an exponential law (T=Ae−krT=Ae−kr) or a power law (T=ArkT=Ark) to model this.

  • You might need to use the absolute temperature, measured in Kelvin. A difference of 1K1K is the same as a difference of 1∘C1∘C but 0∘C≈273K0∘C≈273K.

Use your results to predict the surface temperature of Ceres.
