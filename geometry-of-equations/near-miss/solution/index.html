Explain why the solutions are so different and yet the pairs of equations are nearly identical.

One helpful way to explain this is geometrically. We can view the problem of solving these equations as being the problem of finding the intersections of pairs of lines.

Rewriting these equations in the standard form y=mx+cy=mx+c we see the first pair of equations becomes
yy=−10.99999x+2.999990.99999≈−1.00001x+3.00002=−0.99999x+2.99998,y=−10.99999x+2.999990.99999≈−1.00001x+3.00002y=−0.99999x+2.99998,
 whilst the second gives
yy=−11.00001x+2.999991.00001≈−0.9999900001x+2.99996=−0.99999x+2.99998.y=−11.00001x+2.999991.00001≈−0.9999900001x+2.99996y=−0.99999x+2.99998.

Both pairs of lines are very nearly parallel, but the second pair are much closer to being parallel—even though the difference in their offsets is only a little smaller than that for the first pair.

Because nearly parallel lines approach each other only very slowly, the pair which is much closer to being parallel takes much longer to meet when starting from the origin.

By only very slightly changing the slope (and intercept if we like) of the first line, the point at which the intersection lies can be moved enormous distances. In particular, because two distinct parallel lines never meet, we can make the intersection as far away as we like by slowly tuning the slopes to the same value!

Also, note that in both pairs of equations, the first line is steeper. That said, in the first pair the first line is higher at x=0x=0 whilst in the second pair, the second line is on top at x=0x=0. This explains the difference in the sign of the solutions xx.

As an aside, notice that q=0q=0 is the point around which things become very sensitive in solving qx=pqx=p, which is exactly where the equation might have no solution (or infinitely many solutions).

Similarly, ad−bc=0ad−bc=0 is exactly the point at which the original pair of equations have either no solution or infinitely many solutions. You might like to try to construct simple examples of equations with both zero and infinitely many solutions.
