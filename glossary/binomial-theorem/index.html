Binomial theorem

Printable version

The binomial theorem specifies how we can expand expressions of the form (x+y)n(x+y)n.

In the case that nn is a positive integer, it takes the form
(x+y)n=∑i=0n(ni)xiyn−i=xn+nxn−1y+(n2)xn−2y2+⋯+yn.(x+y)n=∑i=0n(ni)xiyn−i=xn+nxn−1y+(n2)xn−2y2+⋯+yn.

In the general case, where nn is not necessarily a positive integer, we have

(1+x)n=1+nx+n(n−1)2!x2+n(n−1)(n−2)3!x3+⋯(1+x)n=1+nx+n(n−1)2!x2+n(n−1)(n−2)3!x3+⋯
whenever ||x||<1|x|<1.
