Dot product

Printable version

The dot product (or scalar product) of the two real vectors

is
a⋅b=a1b1+a2b2+a3b3.a⋅b=a1b1+a2b2+a3b3.
 This is a real number (hence the name ‘scalar’ product). The definition extends to vectors aa and bb with nn components. (Compare this to the cross product, which outputs a vector and which does not have such a simple generalisation.)

The dot product combines information about the lengths of aa and bb and the angle between them. Specifically,

a⋅b=|a||b|cosθa⋅b=|a||b|cos⁡θ
where |a||a| and |b||b| are the lengths (magnitudes) of aa and bb and θθ is the angle between the two vectors. It follows that for non-zero vectors aa and bb, the dot product is zero exactly when aa and bb are at right angles to one another.
