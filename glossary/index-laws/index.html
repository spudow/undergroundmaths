Index laws

Printable version

The index laws are the rules by which indices (powers) may be combined.

The basic rules are:
a1am⋅an(am)nam⋅bm=a=am+n=amn=(ab)ma1=aam⋅an=am+n(am)n=amnam⋅bm=(ab)m
 From these we can derive other important rules:
a0amana−ma1/n=1=am−n=1am=a⎯⎯√na0=1aman=am−na−m=1ama1/n=an

These rules are valid for aa and bb positive real numbers and mm and nn rational numbers, or aa and bb any numbers and mm and nn integers.
