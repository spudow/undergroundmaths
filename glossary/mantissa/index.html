Glossary

Mantissa

Printable version

The mantissa is the fractional part of a common logarithm (that is, the base 10 logarithm), which represent the digits of the given number but not its order of magnitude.

For example, the mantissa of both log1020≈1.3010log10⁡20≈1.3010 and log10200≈2.3010log10⁡200≈2.3010 is 0.30100.3010.

Note that the mantissa of log100.2≈−0.6990log10⁡0.2≈−0.6990 is also 0.30100.3010. This is because the fractional part must always lie between 00 and 11. In this case, −0.6990=−1+0.3010−0.6990=−1+0.3010, so the integer part is −1−1 while the fractional part is 0.30100.3010.
