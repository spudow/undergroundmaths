Product rule

Printable version

The product rule is a rule for differentiating a product of two functions.

If y=uvy=uv is a product of two functions, then

dydx=dudxv+udvdx.dydx=dudxv+udvdx.

This can be generalised to a product of more than two functions. If y=uvw…y=uvw…, then

dydx=dudxvw…+udvdxw…+uvdwdx…+⋯.dydx=dudxvw…+udvdxw…+uvdwdx…+⋯.

The Leibniz rule is a generalisation of the product rule to second and higher derivatives:

dndxn(uv)=∑k=0n(nk)u(k)v(n−k),dndxn(uv)=∑k=0n(nk)u(k)v(n−k),
where u(k)u(k) means the kkth derivative of uu.
