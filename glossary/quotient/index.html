Glossary

Quotient

Printable version

The quotient is the result of a division, so a/ba/b is called the quotient of aa by bb.

Sometimes, one is interested in the number of times one thing can be divided ‘exactly’ into another, giving a quotient and a remainder.

For example, when working in the integers, 1313 divided by 33 has a quotient of 44 and a remainder of 11.

As another example, x4+x2−xx4+x2−x divided by x2+3x2+3 has a quotient of x2−2x2−2 and a remainder of −x+6−x+6.

If aa divided by bb has a quotient of qq and a remainder of rr, then a=bq+ra=bq+r. Normally, one requires rr to be ‘smaller’ than bb in some sense. When dividing integers, we require 0≤r<|b|0≤r<|b|. When dividing polynomials, we require r=0r=0 or the degree of rr to be less than the degree of bb.

The remainder of a division is 00 if and only if bb is a factor of aa.
