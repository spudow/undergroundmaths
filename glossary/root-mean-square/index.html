Root mean square

Printable version

The root mean square is a type of mean.

Given real numbers a1a1, a2a2, …, anan, the root mean square (often abbreviated to RMS) is obtained by calculating the arithmetic mean of the squares of a1a1, …, anan, and then taking the square root of this:

a21+a22+⋯+a2nn⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√a12+a22+⋯+an2n

It is useful when trying to measure the average “size” of numbers, where their sign is unimportant, as the squaring makes all of the numbers non-negative.

The most common case of using the root mean square is when calculating the standard deviation of a set of numbers x1x1, …, xnxn. The standard deviation is the root mean square of the deviations of these numbers from the mean, that is, the root mean square of (x1−x¯)(x1−x¯), …, (xn−x¯)(xn−x¯), where x¯x¯ is the mean of x1x1, …, xnxn, so

standard deviation=(x1−x¯)2+(x2−x¯)2+⋯+(xn−x¯)2n⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√.standard deviation=(x1−x¯)2+(x2−x¯)2+⋯+(xn−x¯)2n.

The root mean square can also be used for continuous functions, with integration replacing summation. If the function f(x)f(x) is defined for a≤x≤ba≤x≤b, then the root mean square value of f(x)f(x) over this interval is

1b−a∫ba(f(x))2dx⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√.1b−a∫ab(f(x))2dx.

The root mean square is an example of a power mean.
