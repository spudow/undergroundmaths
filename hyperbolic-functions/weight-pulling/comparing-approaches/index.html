Comparing approaches

Approach 1 gave the cartesian equation

x=aln(a+a2−y2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√y)−a2−y2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√.x=aln⁡(a+a2−y2y)−a2−y2.

Approach 2 gave the parametric form

(t−atanh(ta),asech(ta)).(t−atanh⁡(ta),asech⁡(ta)).

Are these equivalent?

In the parametric form, we have

(x,y)=(t−atanh(ta),asech(ta)).(x,y)=(t−atanh⁡(ta),asech⁡(ta)).

Therefore sech(t/a)=y/asech⁡(t/a)=y/a, so a/y=cosh(t/a)a/y=cosh⁡(t/a) and t=aarcosh(a/y)t=aarcosh⁡(a/y).

Using the identity 1−tanh2(t/a)=sech2(t/a)1−tanh2⁡(t/a)=sech2⁡(t/a), we also get

atanh(ta)=a1−(ya)2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√=a2−y2⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯√.atanh⁡(ta)=a1−(ya)2=a2−y2.

This is definitely looking hopeful!

We just need to find a way of expressing arcoshuarcosh⁡u in terms of uu (where we’re writing u=a/yu=a/y to make things look a little simpler).

If arcoshu=warcosh⁡u=w, then u=coshwu=cosh⁡w, so
