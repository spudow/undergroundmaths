Review question

What is the stopping distance for this car?
Add to your resource collection

Ref: R6841
Printable/supporting materials Fullscreen mode Teacher notes

Solution

A car is travelling at 30mph30mph when the driver needs to make an emergency stop. It takes him 0.7seconds0.7seconds to apply the brake and the car comes to rest with uniform retardation in a further 2seconds2seconds. How far has the car travelled since the emergency arose?

We can calculate the distance the car has travelled by drawing a velocity-time graph and calculating the area underneath.

The vertical axis of our graph is the velocity in miles per hour which we need to convert into yards per second. We can convert miles per hour into miles per second by dividing by 36003600 and then multiply by 17601760 to get our answer in yards per second.

303600×1760=443303600×1760=443
We can calculate the area of the rectangle which is the thinking distance,
0.7×443≈10.3.0.7×443≈10.3.
 The area of the triangle is the braking distance,
12×443×2≈14.7.12×443×2≈14.7.
 We can now add our areas together to get our total stopping distance
10.3+14.7=25.010.3+14.7=25.0
 Therefore, since the emergency arose, the car has travelled 25yards25yards.

You could also complete this question by using the constant acceleration formulae.

With the same time taken to apply the brake and the same retardation, what would have been the stopping distance if the car had originally been travelling at 40mph40mph?

We can use the same method for this situation as the only change is the initial velocity. We can work out our new velocity in yards per second as before,

403600×1760=1769.403600×1760=1769.
We can calculate the area of the rectangle,
0.7×1769≈13.7.0.7×1769≈13.7.
 Our old and new triangles both have the same gradient and are therefore similar, so we can use the lengths of the previous triangle to work out the lengths of the new triangle. We know that the scale factor between the triangles is 4343 therefore the length of the base is now
43×2=8343×2=83
 and the area of the triangle is
12×83×1769≈26.1.12×83×1769≈26.1.
 We can now add our areas to get the total distance
13.7+26.1=39.8.13.7+26.1=39.8.
 Therefore the stopping distance if the driver was travelling at 40mph40mph would have been 40yards40yards.

Notice that this is more than 4343 of the 30mph30mph stopping distance. Why is that?
