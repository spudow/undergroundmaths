Review question

Can the trapezium rule help us find the area under y=xlnxy=xln⁡x?

Solution

The continuous function ff maps the real interval
a≤x≤ba≤x≤b

into a subset of the positive real numbers. Derive the trapezium rule for the approximate evaluation of the area under the curve y=f(x)y=f(x) between x=ax=a and x=bx=b, using (n−1)(n−1) points of subdivision at a distance h=(b−a)/nh=(b−a)/n apart.

Consider the following diagram.

In this diagram, we start with the graph of y=f(x)y=f(x) between x=ax=a and x=bx=b. This is shown in black. Our goal is to approximate the area under this curve; this is the region in blue (and purple, where it overlaps with the red region).

We then fix an integer nn, define h=(b−a)/nh=(b−a)/n, and divide the interval a≤x≤ba≤x≤b into nn equal parts (of length hh) by marking the points at a+ha+h, a+2ha+2h, and so on. We then draw line segments between (a,f(a))(a,f(a)) and (a+h,f(a+h))(a+h,f(a+h)); between (a+h,f(a+h))(a+h,f(a+h)) and (a+2h,f(a+2h))(a+2h,f(a+2h)); and so on. This is the (piecewise linear) curve in dark red.

The trapezium rule says that the area under the red curve (the region in light red; this is a sequence of trapezia) is approximately the same as the area under the original graph, with the approximation becoming more exact as nn increases. We will write this symbolically:
area of the first trapezium+area of the second trapezium+⋯+area of the last trapezium=f(a)+f(a+h)2h+f(a+h)+f(a+2h)2h+⋯+f(b−h)+f(b)2h=h2(f(a)+2f(a+h)+2f(a+2h)+⋯+2f(b−h)+f(b))≈∫baf(x)dx.area of the first trapezium+area of the second trapezium+⋯+area of the last trapezium=f(a)+f(a+h)2h+f(a+h)+f(a+2h)2h+⋯+f(b−h)+f(b)2h=h2(f(a)+2f(a+h)+2f(a+2h)+⋯+2f(b−h)+f(b))≈∫abf(x)dx.
Hence obtain an approximation to the integral

using the points of subdivision given in the following table:
