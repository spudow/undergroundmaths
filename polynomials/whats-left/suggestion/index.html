Suggestion

Below is a series of expressions in the form p(x)d(x)p(x)d(x), where p(x)p(x) and d(x)d(x) are polynomials.

What degree are the quotient and remainder polynomials when p(x)p(x) is divided by d(x)d(x)?

A. 3x+1x3x+1x

B. 5x−22x−15x−22x−1

C. 3x2−5x+2x+13x2−5x+2x+1

D. x3−5x2+3x+1x+1x3−5x2+3x+1x+1

E. 5x2+8x+9x2+x+25x2+8x+9x2+x+2

F. x3−x2−7x+1x2+2x−1x3−x2−7x+1x2+2x−1

G. x4−3x3+x+1x−1x4−3x3+x+1x−1

H. 6x4+5x3+x2+10x−32x2+3x−16x4+5x3+x2+10x−32x2+3x−1

Some of the questions have divisors which are quadratic. Below are suggestions as to how methods used for algebraic division by a linear divisor can be extended to working with a quadratic.

E. 5x2+8x+9x2+x+25x2+8x+9x2+x+2

We could rewrite p(x)p(x) so multiples of the divisor, d(x)d(x), appear.

5x2+8x+9x2+x+2=5x2+5x+10+3x−1x2+x+25x2+8x+9x2+x+2=5x2+5x+10+3x−1x2+x+2

Why is this helpful? What might the next step be?

F. x3−x2−7x+1x2+2x−1x3−x2−7x+1x2+2x−1
