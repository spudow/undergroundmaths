Review question

What is this algebraic fraction if its expansion has no xx term?

Solution

In the expansion of

1+3x(1+ax)21+3x(1+ax)2
in ascending powers of xx, the coefficient of xx is zero. Find the value of the constant aa,

We first need to find an expansion for the denominator using the standard binomial expansion.

The binomial theorem says that, if |x|<1|x|<1, then

(1+x)r=1+rx+r(r−1)2!x2+r(r−1)(r−2)3!x3+⋯(1+x)r=1+rx+r(r−1)2!x2+r(r−1)(r−2)3!x3+⋯

In our case, r=−2r=−2 and we have axax in place of xx, so
(1+ax)−2=1−2ax+−2(−3)2!(ax)2+−2(−3)(−4)3!(ax)3+⋯=1−2ax+3a2x2−4a3x3+⋯(1+ax)−2=1−2ax+−2(−3)2!(ax)2+−2(−3)(−4)3!(ax)3+⋯=1−2ax+3a2x2−4a3x3+⋯

The term in xx of the expansion of 1+3x(1+ax)21+3x(1+ax)2 comes from 1×(−2ax)+3x×11×(−2ax)+3x×1. If the coefficient is zero then we know that 3−2a=03−2a=0 so a=32a=32.

…and, when aa has this value, obtain the expansion up to and including the term in x3x3.

Multiplying by the numerator we get

1+3x(1+ax)2=1+(3−2a)x+(3a2−6a)x2+(9a2−4a3)x3+⋯1+3x(1+ax)2=1+(3−2a)x+(3a2−6a)x2+(9a2−4a3)x3+⋯
and substituting for aa we get
1+3x(1+32x)2=1−94x2+274x3 up to and including the term in x3.1+3x(1+32x)2=1−94x2+274x3 up to and including the term in x3.

Note that the condition for the expansion to be valid is ||ax||<1|ax|<1 which means that ||x||<23|x|<23.
