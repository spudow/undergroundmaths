Product Rule & Integration by Parts
Review question

How could we integrate e−xsinnxe−xsinn⁡x?

Solution

  1. Show that ddx(cosxsinn−1x)=(n−1)sinn−2x−nsinnxddx(cos⁡xsinn−1⁡x)=(n−1)sinn−2⁡x−nsinn⁡x.

How can we differentiate a product?

What techniques could we use to remove any occurrence of cosxcos⁡x from the resulting expression?

Differentiating using the product rule gives
ddx(cosxsinn−1x)=−sinxsinn−1x+(n−1)cos2xsinn−2x,ddx(cos⁡xsinn−1⁡x)=−sin⁡xsinn−1⁡x+(n−1)cos2⁡xsinn−2⁡x,
 and then by using the identity cos2x=1−sin2xcos2⁡x=1−sin2⁡x we get
ddx(cosxsinn−1x)=−sinnx+(n−1)(1−sin2x)sinn−2x=(n−1)sinn−2x−nsinnx,ddx(cos⁡xsinn−1⁡x)=−sinn⁡x+(n−1)(1−sin2⁡x)sinn−2⁡x=(n−1)sinn−2⁡x−nsinn⁡x,

as required.

Writing s=sinxs=sin⁡x and c=cosxc=cos⁡x can be helpful. We have
ddx(csn−1)=−ssn−1+(n−1)c2sn−2.ddx(csn−1)=−ssn−1+(n−1)c2sn−2.
 Using c2=1−s2c2=1−s2 we get
ddx(csn−1)=−sn+(n−1)(1−s2)sn−2=(n−1)sn−2−nsn=(n−1)sinn−2x−nsinnxddx(csn−1)=−sn+(n−1)(1−s2)sn−2=(n−1)sn−2−nsn=(n−1)sinn−2⁡x−nsinn⁡x

as required.

  1. Given that In=∫12π0e−xsinnxdxIn=∫012πe−xsinn⁡xdx, show that
    In=−e−12π+n∫12π0e−xcosxsinn−1xdx,(n≥1).In=−e−12π+n∫012πe−xcos⁡xsinn−1⁡xdx,(n≥1).

Here we want to integrate by parts (our ‘product rule’ for integration).

The general formula for integration by parts is

∫baudvdxdx=[uv]ba−∫bavdudxdx.∫abudvdxdx=[uv]ab−∫abvdudxdx.

We know how to integrate e−xe−x and differentiate sinnxsinn⁡x, so we take u=sinnxu=sinn⁡x and dvdx=e−xdvdx=e−x, giving v=−e−xv=−e−x. Then integration by parts yields
In=[−e−xsinnx]12π0−∫12π0−e−xnsinn−1xcosxdx,In=[−e−xsinn⁡x]012π−∫012π−e−xnsinn−1⁡xcos⁡xdx,
 which, after evaluating the limits, becomes
In=−e−12π+n∫12π0e−xcosxsinn−1xdx.(1)(1)In=−e−12π+n∫012πe−xcos⁡xsinn−1⁡xdx.
  1. By using the results of (i) and (ii), or otherwise, show that
    (n2+1)In=−e−12π+n(n−1)In−2,(n≥2).(n2+1)In=−e−12π+n(n−1)In−2,(n≥2).
Integrating the integral in (1)(1) by parts, taking u=cosxsinn−1xu=cos⁡xsinn−1⁡x and dvdx=e−xdvdx=e−x (and noticing that we’ve already worked out dudxdudx in part (i)) gives
∫12π0e−xcosxsinn−1xdx=[−e−xcosxsinn−1x]12π0+∫12π0e−x((n−1)sinn−2x−nsinnx)dx=(n−1)∫12π0e−xsinn−2xdx−n∫12π0e−xsinnxdx=(n−1)In−2−nIn∫012πe−xcos⁡xsinn−1⁡xdx=[−e−xcos⁡xsinn−1⁡x]012π+∫012πe−x((n−1)sinn−2⁡x−nsinn⁡x)dx=(n−1)∫012πe−xsinn−2⁡xdx−n∫012πe−xsinn⁡xdx=(n−1)In−2−nIn

(The third line follows by evaluating the limits, which are both zero as long as n>1n>1; we are specifically told n≥2n≥2 here.)

Substituting this into (1)(1) now gives

In=−e−12π+n(n−1)In−2−n2In.In=−e−12π+n(n−1)In−2−n2In.

Rearranging gives
(n2+1)In=−e−12π+n(n−1)In−2(2)(2)(n2+1)In=−e−12π+n(n−1)In−2

as required.

  1. Show that I4=185(24−41e−12π)I4=185(24−41e−12π).

Rearranging (2)(2) gives

In=1n2+1(−e−12π+n(n−1)In−2).In=1n2+1(−e−12π+n(n−1)In−2).

So by setting n=4n=4 we get

I4=117(−e−12π+12I2),I4=117(−e−12π+12I2),
and similarly, setting n=2n=2 gives
I2=15(−e−12π+2I0).I2=15(−e−12π+2I0).

By substituting the expression for I2I2 into that for I4I4 we get

I4=117(−e−12π+12(15(−e−12π+2I0))),I4=117(−e−12π+12(15(−e−12π+2I0))),
which rearranges to give
I4=185(24I0−17e−12π).I4=185(24I0−17e−12π).

I0=∫12π0e−xdx=[−e−x]12π0=1−e−12π,I0=∫012πe−xdx=[−e−x]012π=1−e−12π,

so the expression for I4I4 becomes
