Solution

Take any two points AA and BB on the parabola y=x2y=x2.

Draw the line OCOC through the origin, parallel to ABAB, cutting the parabola again at CC.

Let AA have coordinates (a,a2)(a,a2), let BB have coordinates (b,b2)(b,b2) and let CC have coordinates (c,c2)(c,c2).

Prove that a+b=ca+b=c.

We know that

A=(a,a2)A=(a,a2),

B=(b,b2)B=(b,b2),

C=(c,c2)C=(c,c2).

We can calculate the gradient of a straight line between two points (x1,y1)(x1,y1) and (x2,y2)(x2,y2) as y1−y2x1−x2y1−y2x1−x2.

So the gradient of line ABAB is equal to a2−b2a−b=a+ba2−b2a−b=a+b.

Also, the gradient of line OCOC is equal to 0−c20−c=c0−c20−c=c.

However, we know from the question that these two lines are parallel. Two lines are parallel if and only if they have the same gradient, which means that we must have a+b=ca+b=c.

Imagine drawing another parallel line DEDE, where DD and EE are two other points on the parabola. Extend the ideas of the previous result to prove that the midpoints of each of the three parallel lines lie on a straight line.

Now, suppose that we have two points D=(d,d2)D=(d,d2) and E=(e,e2)E=(e,e2) on the parabola making another line parallel to OCOC.

The gradient of line DEDE is equal to d2−e2d−e=d+ed2−e2d−e=d+e. As DEDE is parallel to OCOC, we also have d+e=cd+e=c.

We can calculate the midpoint of a straight line between two points (x1,y1)(x1,y1) and (x2,y2)(x2,y2) as (x1+x22,y1+y22)(x1+x22,y1+y22).

So the midpoint of line OCOC is

(0+c2,0+c22)=(c2,c22).(0+c2,0+c22)=(c2,c22).

The midpoint of line ABAB is

(a+b2,a2+b22)=(c2,a2+b22).(a+b2,a2+b22)=(c2,a2+b22).

The midpoint of line DEDE is

(d+e2,d2+e22)=(c2,d2+e22).(d+e2,d2+e22)=(c2,d2+e22).

All three of these points have an xx-coordinate of c2c2. This means that the line x=c2x=c2 passes through the midpoints of the lines OCOC, ABAB and DEDE.
