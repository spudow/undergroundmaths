Review question

How fast are these particles sliding when they pass?

Solution

Two particles AA and BB are placed side by side on rough horizontal ground, and are simultaneously projected along the ground in the same direction with speeds 14ms−114ms−1 and 21ms−121ms−1 respectively. AA comes to rest after travelling 70m70m, and BB after travelling 35m35m. Calculate the coefficient of friction between each particle and the ground, and show that the times for which the particles are in motion are in the ratio 3:13:1.

Take gg to be 9.8ms−29.8ms−2.

Let’s start by drawing a diagram.

The particles are moving so friction is a maximum and we can use the relationship F=μRF=μR, where RR is the normal reaction and μμ is the coefficient of friction. FF is the only horizontal force on the object.

We know R=mgR=mg and Newton’s 3rd law tells us that F=maF=ma leading to μ=a/gμ=a/g (where aa is directed to the left, in the same direction as the frictional force).

To find the coefficient of friction for each particle we must first find its acceleration. For this we can use the equation v2=u2+2asv2=u2+2as.

aA=0−1422×70=−196140=−75aA=0−1422×70=−196140=−75 so μA=75g=17μA=75g=17.

aB=0−2122×35=−44170=−6310aB=0−2122×35=−44170=−6310 so μB=6310g=914μB=6310g=914.

To find out the time of travel we can use v=u+atv=u+at.

tA=0−14−7/5=10tA=0−14−7/5=10 and tB=0−21−63/10=103tB=0−21−63/10=103.

So the ratio tA:tB=10:103=3:1tA:tB=10:103=3:1 as required.

Show also that AA passes BB before either particle comes to rest, and calculate the speed of each particle when this happens.

Using s=ut+12at2s=ut+12at2 to find distances at time tt,
sAsB=14t−710t2=21t−6320t2.sA=14t−710t2sB=21t−6320t2.

They pass when sA=sBsA=sB, so subtracting the above equations, 7t−4920t2=07t−4920t2=0 which means t=0t=0 (i.e. the start) or t=207t=207.

At this time AA has speed 14−75×207=10ms−114−75×207=10ms−1 and BB has speed 21−6310×207=3ms−121−6310×207=3ms−1. This is clearly before either particle has come to rest.
