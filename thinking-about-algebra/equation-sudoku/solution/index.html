Sudoku 1

Here is one way we could solve this puzzle.

Here are the 16 equations:

  1. c+m+h=19c+m+h=19

  2. f+e=10f+e=10

  3. k+g+m+c=23k+g+m+c=23

  4. g+p=11g+p=11

  5. h+f=14h+f=14

  6. g+m=13g+m=13

  7. a+e+k+h=11a+e+k+h=11

  8. k+c+f+a=22k+c+f+a=22

  9. c+g+k=17c+g+k=17

  10. f+g+a=19f+g+a=19

  11. m+k+c=16m+k+c=16

  12. p+e=5p+e=5

  13. g+m+f=22g+m+f=22

  14. h+m+a=14h+m+a=14

  15. e+c+h+k=16e+c+h+k=16

  16. f+h=14f+h=14

We first look at the most simple equations with only two unknowns.

Equation 12 says that p+e=5p+e=5. This means that both pp and ee must be less than or equal to 44.

Equation 2 says that f+e=10f+e=10, and since we know that ee is less than or equal to 44, this means that f≥6f≥6.

Equation 4 says that g+p=11g+p=11, so we must have g≥7g≥7.

Equation 5/16 tells us that h+f=14h+f=14, so h≤8h≤8.

Now look at equation 7, a+e+k+h=11a+e+k+h=11. The only four different positive whole numbers that can add up to 1111 are 1+2+3+51+2+3+5. So we must have that each of aa, ee, kk, hh must be one of 11, 22, 33, 55. We know that e≤4e≤4, but we now know that e≤3e≤3, and so 2≤p≤42≤p≤4. Also, since h≤8h≤8 and must add up with a number less than or equal to 99 to make 1414 by equation 5, we have that

h=5.h=5.
So by equation 5,
f=9.f=9.
 But now by equation 2
e=1,e=1,
 and by equation 12
p=4,p=4,
 which we can put into equation 4 to find that
g=7.g=7.
 We now use equation 6 to deduce that
m=6.m=6.

It remains to deduce the values of the letters aa and kk (each of which we know must take one of the values 22 and 33) and cc. The only number left for cc is therefore 88—we can check using equation 1

c+m+h=c+6+5=19⟹c=8✓c+m+h=c+6+5=19⟹c=8✓

We use equation 14 to check what value aa takes:

h+m+a=5+6+a=14⟹a=3.h+m+a=5+6+a=14⟹a=3.

So we must have k=2k=2, but we can double check: by equation 11,

m+k+c=6+k+8=16⟹k=2✓m+k+c=6+k+8=16⟹k=2✓

Sudoku 2

For the second Sudoku, we have the following 10 equations:

  1. m+n=am+n=a

  2. g+n+f=g+c⟹n+f=cg+n+f=g+c⟹n+f=c

  3. g+d=ng+d=n

  4. g+m=m+f+d⟹g=f+dg+m=m+f+d⟹g=f+d

  5. g+c+e=a+e⟹g+c=ag+c+e=a+e⟹g+c=a

  6. b+g+f=a+g⟹b+f=ab+g+f=a+g⟹b+f=a

  7. n+g+c=b+c⟹n+g=bn+g+c=b+c⟹n+g=b

  8. a+d+e=2a⟹d+e=aa+d+e=2a⟹d+e=a

  9. g+f+a=m+a⟹g+f=mg+f+a=m+a⟹g+f=m

  10. e+n+m=a+b+de+n+m=a+b+d

Equations 3 and 4 share two of the same unknowns, so this seems like a good place to start. Eliminating gg yields the equation

f+d=n−d⟹n=2d+f.f+d=n−d⟹n=2d+f.

From equation 2, n=c−fn=c−f, so we further see that

c−f=2d+f⟹c=2d+2f.c−f=2d+f⟹c=2d+2f.
From this equation we see that cc is even, and c≥6c≥6 (since the smallest values dd and ff can take are 11 and 22), so we have that either c=6c=6 and the dd and ff are equal to 11 and two or c=8c=8 and then dd and ff are equal to 11 and 33.

Now look at equation 5, g+c=ag+c=a. The largest value that gg can take is 33, if c=6c=6. The only other option therefore, is that g=2g=2, since we have already established that one of dd and ff must be 11. But since we must have that a≤9a≤9, this means that we must have

c=6,c=6,
and therefore each of dd and ff must take one of the values 11 and 22. This excludes the possibility of gg taking the value 22, so we must have
g=3,g=3,
 which tells us that
a=9a=9
 by equation 5.

Equation 1 says that m+n=a=9m+n=a=9. Since the values 11 to 33 are already taken, the only way we can make 99 with two numbers is with 44 and 55. So each of mm and nn must take one of the values 44 and 55. The other letters and numbers we have left are bb and ee and 77 and 88. We will look at equation 10, e+n+m=a+b+de+n+m=a+b+d to determine which values they should take. We know that n+m=a=9n+m=a=9, so we can simplify this equation to consider e=b+de=b+d, which says that b<eb<e, so since we knew each of bb and ee were one of 77 and 88, we now know that

b=7 and e=8,b=7 and e=8,
which further tells us that we must have
d=1,d=1,
 and so we must also have
f=2.f=2.
 Equation 3 now says that 3+1=n3+1=n, so we finally know that
n=4 and m=5.n=4 and m=5.
