Thinking about Algebra
Review question

If these equations have an infinity of solutions, how are aa and bb related?

Solution

Given that aa and bb are non-zero constants, and that the equations

a2x+abyabx+b2y=a+4b=a+ba2x+aby=a+4babx+b2y=a+b

have an infinite number of solutions for xx and yy, express aa in terms of bb.

For given values of aa and bb, a2x+aby=a+4ba2x+aby=a+4b and abx+b2y=a+babx+b2y=a+b represent straight lines in the x−yx−y plane.

Approach 1

Multiply the first equation by bb, and the second equation by aa. Now subtract — we find that

ba2x+ab2y−a2bx−ab2y=ab+4b2−a2−ba.ba2x+ab2y−a2bx−ab2y=ab+4b2−a2−ba.

So all the xx and yy terms cancel, giving us that (if the equations are to be consistent) 4b2−a2=04b2−a2=0.

So the set of equations has either no solutions (the lines are parallel, and the equations are inconsistent) or an infinite number of solutions (the lines are on top of each other, and the equations are consistent).

So for an infinite number of solutions we need 0=4b2−a20=4b2−a2 to be true, which leads us to

a=±2b.a=±2b.

Approach 2

We can re-write each equation in the form y=mx+cy=mx+c ( dividing by aa and bb is not a problem, since they are non-zero). We have

y=−axb+4a+1b,y=−axb+ab2+1b.y=−axb+4a+1b,y=−axb+ab2+1b.

We require these straight lines to coincide (be on top of each other). Their gradients are equal, so this can only happen if their yy-intercepts are the same.

This means we need

4a+1b=ab2+1b,4a+1b=ab2+1b,

which yields

a=±2b.a=±2b.
