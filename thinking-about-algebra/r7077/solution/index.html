Solution

  1. The variables xx and yy are connected by the relation y=axny=axn, where aa and nn are constants; y=3y=3 when x=4x=4 and y=2y=2 when x=9x=9. Find the exact values of nn and aa.
By substituting the information we’re given in the question, we obtain the equations
3=4na(1)(1)3=4na
 and
2=9na.(2)(2)2=9na.

Dividing the second by the first to eliminate aa gives

23=(94)n.23=(94)n.

To work with this, it will be more convenient to write everything as powers of 22 and 33.

That is, 22n+1=32n+122n+1=32n+1, which can only be the case when 2n+1=02n+1=0 (as 22 and 33 have highest common factor 11).

Therefore, n=−12n=−12.

Now we can go back to equation (1)(1) or equation (2)(2) to find that a=6a=6.

Alternatively, if you know about logarithms…

Taking logs to base 1010 with our starting equations (any base will do, in fact) we get

log3=loga+2nlog2,log2=loga+2nlog3.log⁡3=log⁡a+2nlog⁡2,log⁡2=log⁡a+2nlog⁡3.

Subtracting these gives log3−log2=2n(log2−log3)log⁡3−log⁡2=2n(log⁡2−log⁡3), and so n=−12n=−12.

Substituting back into one of our starting equations gives a=6a=6.
