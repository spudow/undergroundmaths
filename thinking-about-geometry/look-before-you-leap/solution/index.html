Solution

The diagonals of the square ABCDABCD meet at OO.

The bisector of angle OABOAB meets BOBO and BCBC at NN and PP respectively.

The length of NONO is 2424.

How long is PCPC?

There are many ways to solve this problem. We’ll give one solution in detail here, and suggest some other approaches that you could investigate as you meet new ideas later on the Geometry line.

Method 1

The question doesn’t tell us the side length of the square, but it seems like that might be an important quantity so let’s give it a name and see whether it’s relevant later on.

Let the square have side length xx. Then the diagonals have length x2⎯⎯√x2, and

BO=x2⎯⎯√2=x2⎯⎯√.BO=x22=x2.

We see that triangles AONAON and ABPABP are similar (they both have a right angle and one of the other angles is 12∠BAO12∠BAO so they have the same angles).

Here’s a tip when dealing with similar triangles. When we said that triangles AONAON and ABPABP are similar, the points were in the right order. That is, vertex AA in triangle AONAON corresponds to vertex AA in triangle ABPABP, vertex OO in triangle AONAON corresponds to vertex BB in triangle ABPABP, and vertex NN in triangle AONAON corresponds to vertex PP in triangle ABPABP. Being careful about that ordering when we wrote down the similar triangles makes it easier to be clear about which sides of the triangles correspond.

Now

BPAB=ONAO,BPAB=ONAO,
so
BP=AB×ONAO=24xx/2⎯⎯√=242⎯⎯√.BP=AB×ONAO=24xx/2=242.

The triangle BNPBNP is interesting because it involves the lengths BNBN and BPBP, which are important in this question. Let’s think about the angles in that triangle.

We have ∠BAP=∠PAC=22.5∘∠BAP=∠PAC=22.5∘ (because APAP is the angle bisector of angle BACBAC, and that is 45∘45∘). Now triangle BAPBAP is right-angled, and the sum of the angles in the triangle is 180∘180∘, so

∠BPA=180∘−90∘−22.5∘=67.5∘.∠BPA=180∘−90∘−22.5∘=67.5∘.
We can also find ∠BNP∠BNP, as we know that ∠NBP=45∘∠NBP=45∘. Looking at the angles in triangle BNPBNP, we have
∠BNP=180∘−∠NBP−∠BPN=67.5∘.∠BNP=180∘−∠NBP−∠BPN=67.5∘.

We have shown that ∠BPA=∠BNP∠BPA=∠BNP, so triangle BNPBNP is isosceles, so BN=BP=242⎯⎯√BN=BP=242 (using our earlier calculation).

We could use this to find the length xx (using BN+NO=BOBN+NO=BO and our expressions for those three lengths), but in fact we do not need to find xx in order to complete the question.

We see that triangles APCAPC and ANBANB are similar (they each contain an angle of 45∘45∘ and an angle of 22.5∘22.5∘, so have all angles the same), so

PCAC=NBAB,PCAC=NBAB,
so
PC=AC×NBAB=x2⎯⎯√×242⎯⎯√x=48.PC=AC×NBAB=x2×242x=48.

Here is another argument using similar triangles. The argument is perhaps simpler and more elegant, but involves thinking of constructing another point and line.

Method 2

Let RR be the point of intersection of the line APAP with the line through CC parallel to ONON.

Then ∠ACR=90∘∠ACR=90∘, so triangles AONAON and ACRACR are both right-angled, and they share the angle at AA, so their angles are the same and so they are similar. This shows that

CRON=ACAO=2,CRON=ACAO=2,
so CR=2×ON=2×24=48CR=2×ON=2×24=48.

It’s not clear that this will be helpful, unless we can find a way to relate CRCR to PCPC…

We haven’t yet used the fact that the line APAP bisects the angle BAOBAO, so we should try to use that.

Since the line APAP is the bisector of angle BAOBAO, we see that ∠NAO=22.5∘∠NAO=22.5∘, and so

∠ANO=∠ARC=180∘−90∘−22.5∘=67.5∘.∠ANO=∠ARC=180∘−90∘−22.5∘=67.5∘.
Also,
∠RPC=180∘−∠PRC−∠PCR=180∘−67.5∘−(90∘−45∘)=67.5∘.∠RPC=180∘−∠PRC−∠PCR=180∘−67.5∘−(90∘−45∘)=67.5∘.

This means that triangle CRPCRP is isosceles, and so

PC=RC=48.PC=RC=48.

Here are some starting points for other possible approaches.

Method 3

Write [XYZ][XYZ] for the area of triangle XYZXYZ.

Triangles BAPBAP and PACPAC have the same height, so their areas are in the same ratio as their bases:

[BAP][PAC]=BPPC.[BAP][PAC]=BPPC.
But we can also compute the areas in another way, using the ‘12absinC12absin⁡C’ formula. For convenience, let θ=∠BAPθ=∠BAP (so θ=22.5∘θ=22.5∘, although that does not matter here). We get
[BAP][BAC]=12AB×APsinθ12AP×ACsinθ.[BAP][BAC]=12AB×APsin⁡θ12AP×ACsin⁡θ.

Can you complete the argument?

Method 4

Triangle ABPABP is right-angled, so we can use trigonometry. Since ∠BAP=22.5∘∠BAP=22.5∘, we see that

BP=ABtan(22.5∘).BP=ABtan⁡(22.5∘).
We can use a double angle formula for tan(2θ)tan⁡(2θ) to find tan(22.5∘)tan⁡(22.5∘), since we know the value of tan(45∘)tan⁡(45∘).

Can you complete the argument?
