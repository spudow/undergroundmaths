What is special about the denominators here?

How would you rationalise fractions in the following form: ab⎯⎯√ab, abc⎯⎯√abc and ab+c⎯⎯√ab+c?

  • The simplest way to rationalise ab⎯⎯√ab would be to multiply by b⎯⎯√b⎯⎯√bb, giving ab⎯⎯√b.abb.

  • For abc⎯⎯√abc we could write: abc⎯⎯√×c⎯⎯√c⎯⎯√=ac⎯⎯√bc.abc×cc=acbc.

  • For denominators that contain 2 terms such as ab+c⎯⎯√,ab+c, the examples above showed that multiplying by the difference of two squares would rationalise the denominator, as of the terms containing surds will cancel with each other: ab+c⎯⎯√×b−c⎯⎯√b−c⎯⎯√=ab−ac⎯⎯√b2−c.ab+c×b−cb−c=ab−acb2−c.

Is there more than one way to rationalise a fraction?

We have seen we can rationalise 253⎯⎯√253 by multiplying by 3⎯⎯√3⎯⎯√33. What happens if you try and rationalise by multiplying by 23⎯⎯√23⎯⎯√2323?
