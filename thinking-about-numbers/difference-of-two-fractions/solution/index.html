Solution

The following eight unit fractions are given:

12131415161718191213141516171819

  • What is the probability that a pair of distinct fractions, chosen at random from these, have a difference that is also a unit fraction?

If I consider every possible pair of cards that I might select and record their differences I obtain the following results:

1212 1313 1414 1515 1616 1717 1818 1919
1212 X 1616 1414 310310 1313 514514 3838 718718
1313 1616 X 112112 215215 1616 421421 524524 2929
1414 1414 112112 X 120120 112112 328328 1818 536536
1515 310310 215215 120120 X 130130 235235 340340 445445
1616 1313 1616 112112 130130 X 142142 124124 118118
1717 514514 421421 328328 235235 142142 X 156156 263263
1818 3838 524524 1818 340340 124124 156156 X 172172
1919 718718 2929 536536 445445 118118 263263 172172 X

From this I can see that there are 5656 pairs of fractions I could pick (where I treat picking 1212 first and then 1313 as different from picking 1313 first and then 1212), 2828 of which have differences which are unit fractions. Therefore the probability that a pair of distinct fractions, chosen at random from this list, have a difference that is a unit fraction is 2856=122856=12.

  • What can you say about the pairs of fractions that have a difference that is a unit fraction?

  • Can you generalise your findings?

When I was completing the table of differences I noticed that there are two ‘types’ of fraction pairs that have a difference that is a unit fraction. First, there are those that ‘immediately’ result in a unit fraction, for example

12−13=36−26=16.12−13=36−26=16.
Then there are those that result in a unit fraction once simplified, for example
12−16=36−16=26,12−16=36−16=26,
 which then simplifies to the unit fraction 1313.

If I consider those pairs that ‘immediately’ result in a unit fraction, then I can look for any patterns:
12131414161618and13,and14,and15,and18,and17,and19,and19.121314151617and14,and16,and16,and16,and18,and18,12and13,12and14,13and14,13and16,14and15,14and16,14and18,15and16,16and17,16and18,16and19,17and18,18and19.

Among these, I notice that there are lots of pairs of fractions whose denominators are consecutive. In fact, every possible such pair appears in this list, so it looks as if whenever two unit fractions have denominators that are consecutive then their difference will be a unit fraction. In order to decide whether this is always true I need to prove it.

Two unit fractions with consecutive denominators can be represented as

1nand1n+1.1nand1n+1.
In order to find the difference between these two fractions I need to find a common denominator. In this case I might chose to multiply the denominators together giving n(n+1)n(n+1) as the common denominator.

Writing each fraction with a denominator of n(n+1)n(n+1) gives

1n=n+1n(n+1)and1n+1=nn(n+1).1n=n+1n(n+1)and1n+1=nn(n+1).

I can now find the difference between the two fractions

n+1n(n+1)−nn(n+1)=1n(n+1),n+1n(n+1)−nn(n+1)=1n(n+1),
which is a unit fraction!

I’m not yet sure what to do with the other pairs of fractions, though. There are some patterns with some of them, but not all. I think I’ll pick some of those where I can spot a pattern, and leave the rest for the time being.

Now I’ll consider the other pairs of fractions that have a difference that is a unit fraction. I notice that for some of these pairs, the denominator of one is half (or double) the other. For example, 1212 and 1414; 1313 and 1616; 1414 and 1818 all have a difference that is a unit fraction.

I could represent a pair of unit fractions of this form as

1nand12n.1nand12n.
Then finding the difference I have
1n−12n=22n−12n=12n1n−12n=22n−12n=12n
 which is a unit fraction. So this type of pair always works.

I still have some pairs of fractions to consider, some which have a difference that is a unit fraction once simplified, others of which give a unit fraction straight away. As it’s not clear to me what to do with the remaining examples in the above list, I’ll shift my attention to the one example we have which gives a unit fraction once simplified.

There is one pair of fractions:

12and16,12and16,
which gives a unit fraction after simplification. How does this happen?

We have

12−16=36−16=26=13.12−16=36−16=26=13.
After pondering for a bit, I’ve noticed that the factor of 22 which cancelled at the end was a factor of the original denominators, and in fact was their highest common factor (HCF). That’s making me look back at the original list of fraction pairs to see if there are any other examples where the denominators have a highest common factor of 22.

And indeed there are! The pairs

14and16;16and1814and16;16and18
also have this property.

So we can rewrite the three pairs of fractions from above as follows:
12×112×212×3and12×3,and12×3,and12×4,12×1and12×3,12×2and12×3,12×3and12×4,

We could even add 1212 and 1414 to this list, even though it is in the form 1n1n and 12n12n, which we have already dealt with.

These pairs of fractions are in the form 12a12a and 12b12b, where aa and bb are coprime.

Calculating the difference between this pair of general fractions gives

12a−12b=b2ab−a2ab=b−a2ab.12a−12b=b2ab−a2ab=b−a2ab.
This will be a unit fraction if b−a=1b−a=1 or if b−ab−a is a factor of 2ab2ab.

This explains all of the examples here: for the first pair, a=1a=1 and b=3b=3 differ by 22; for the other pairs (a=2a=2 and b=3b=3; a=3a=3 and b=4b=4), the difference is b−a=1b−a=1.

What possible values could b−ab−a take if this does simplify to a unit fraction?

The result is a unit fraction if, and only if, b−ab−a is a factor of 22.

If b−ab−a is a factor of 22, then the fraction certainly simplifies to a unit fraction.

On the other hand, if the fraction does simplify, then b−ab−a must be a factor of 2ab2ab, as we have said.

Since aa and bb are coprime, b−ab−a and aa must also be coprime. For if d>1d>1 is a factor of both b−ab−a and aa, then dd would also be a factor of (b−a)+a=b(b−a)+a=b, so dd would be a common factor of aa and bb. But this is impossible as aa and bb are coprime.

Likewise, b−ab−a and bb are coprime. So if b−ab−a is a factor of 2ab2ab, it must be the case that b−ab−a is a factor of 22.

Therefore we will end up with a unit fraction if b−a=1b−a=1 or b−a=2b−a=2. Furthermore, if b−a=2b−a=2, then both aa and bb must be odd. (Why?)

More ideas along these lines are explored at Divisibility & Induction.

One pair of fractions remains unaccounted for: 1616 and 1919. These denominators have an HCF of 33, so it might be interesting to think in general about a pair of unit fractions that have denominators with an HCF other than 11 or 22.

A pair of fractions that have an HCF of cc can be written in the form

1acand1bc1acand1bc
where aa and bb are coprime. Calculating their difference I find that
1ac−1bc=b−aabc.1ac−1bc=b−aabc.
 I see that, as before, this will be a unit fraction if and only if b−a=1b−a=1 or if b−ab−a is a factor of abcabc. In fact, as 11 is a factor of cc, we can just say that this will be a unit fraction if and only if b−ab−a is a factor of cc.

As before, what possible values could b−ab−a take if this does simplify to a unit fraction?

Similarly to above (with essentially the same argument), it must be the case that b−ab−a is a factor of cc, as aa and bb are coprime.

Now I understand what I first observed. If I write my pair of unit fractions as

1acand1bc1acand1bc
where cc is the HCF of the denominators, so that aa and bb are coprime, then this will simplify to a unit fraction if b−ab−a is a factor of abcabc (in fact, if it is a factor of cc). It will give a unit fraction immediately if b−a=1b−a=1, that is, if the denominators differ by their highest common factor, or are consecutive multiples of their HCF. They will give one after simplification if b−a>1b−a>1, but is still a factor of their HCF.

  • The fractions in this problem are all unit fractions. Can you find a pair of fractions, abab and cdcd where a≠1a≠1 and c≠1c≠1, which have a difference that is a unit fraction?

  • Can you expand on your answer to the previous question – under what conditions will a pair of fractions, abab and cdcd where a≠1a≠1 and c≠1c≠1, have a difference that is a unit fraction?

Some examples I found are
11711289and32,and51,and910.117and32,112and51,89and910.

If I consider the more general fractions abab and cdcd then I can identify the conditions under which they will have a difference that is a unit fraction.

Calculating the difference between the two general fractions gives

ab−cd=ad−bcbd.ab−cd=ad−bcbd.
For this to be a unit fraction I require that ad−bc=1ad−bc=1 or that ad−bcad−bc is a factor of bdbd, so that the difference will simplify to a unit fraction.
