Trigonometry: Compound Angles
Review question

Can we find the value of sin18∘sin⁡18∘ as a surd?

Solution

  1. Show that θ=18∘θ=18∘ satisfies the equation cos3θ=sin2θcos⁡3θ=sin⁡2θ.

We know that

cos(3×18∘)=cos54∘=sin(90∘−54∘)=sin36∘=sin(2×18∘).cos⁡(3×18∘)=cos⁡54∘=sin⁡(90∘−54∘)=sin⁡36∘=sin⁡(2×18∘).
So θ=18∘θ=18∘ satisfies
cos3θ=sin2θ.cos⁡3θ=sin⁡2θ.

By expressing cos3θcos⁡3θ and sin2θsin⁡2θ in terms of trigonometrical functions of θθ, show that sin18∘sin⁡18∘ is a root of the equation

4x2+2x−1=0,4x2+2x−1=0,
…

We can express both cos3θcos⁡3θ and sin2θsin⁡2θ in terms of just s=sinθs=sin⁡θ and c=cosθc=cos⁡θ.

We’ll use the identities

sin2θ=2sc,sin⁡2θ=2sc,
cos2θ=1−2s2cos⁡2θ=1−2s2
 and
cos(α+β)=cosαcosβ−sinαsinβ.cos⁡(α+β)=cos⁡αcos⁡β−sin⁡αsin⁡β.

We have

cos3θ=cos(2θ+θ)=cos(2θ)c−sin(2θ)s=(1−2s2)c−2scs=c(1−4s2).cos⁡3θ=cos⁡(2θ+θ)=cos⁡(2θ)c−sin⁡(2θ)s=(1−2s2)c−2scs=c(1−4s2).

So if sin2θ−cos3θ=0sin⁡2θ−cos⁡3θ=0, we have 2sc−c(1−4s2)=02sc−c(1−4s2)=0, or c(4s2+2s−1)=0.c(4s2+2s−1)=0.

Now we know that sin(18∘)sin⁡(18∘) is a solution to this equation, and so sin(18∘)sin⁡(18∘) is a root of 4x2+2x−1=04x2+2x−1=0, since cos18∘≠0cos⁡18∘≠0.

Hence find its value as a surd.

We complete the square in order to find the roots of 4x2+2x−14x2+2x−1 in surd form. We have

4x2+2x−1=(2x+12)2−54=0,4x2+2x−1=(2x+12)2−54=0,
so that
2x+12=±5⎯⎯√2,2x+12=±52,
 and we have
x=−1±5⎯⎯√4.x=−1±54.
 Finally, we must choose whether we need a plus or a minus sign to find the root corresponding to sin18∘sin⁡18∘. We know that sin18∘>0sin⁡18∘>0, so we choose the plus sign. So we have that
sin18∘=−1+5⎯⎯√4.sin⁡18∘=−1+54.

Find all solutions between 0∘0∘ and 360∘360∘ of the equation

4tan2x=cotx.4tan⁡2x=cot⁡x.

We play a similar game here to that of the first part of the question. Firstly, we can write

tanx=sc,cotx=1tanx=cs,tan⁡x=sc,cot⁡x=1tan⁡x=cs,
so the equation we need to solve becomes
4sin(2x)cos(2x)=cs⟹4sin(2x)s−cos(2x)c=0⟹8s2c−(1−2s2)c=0⟹(10s2−1)c=0.4sin⁡(2x)cos⁡(2x)=cs⟹4sin⁡(2x)s−cos⁡(2x)c=0⟹8s2c−(1−2s2)c=0⟹(10s2−1)c=0.
 So we have that cosx=0cos⁡x=0 solves this, which gives us the values x=90∘,270∘x=90∘,270∘ between 0∘0∘ and 360∘360∘. We also have that
sinx=±110⎯⎯⎯⎯√sin⁡x=±110
 is a solution, so that x=sin−1(±1/10⎯⎯⎯⎯√)x=sin−1⁡(±1/10) are also solutions. To work out how many solutions between 0∘0∘ and 360∘360∘, consider

We see that sinx=±1/10⎯⎯⎯⎯√sin⁡x=±1/10 four times between 0∘0∘ and 360∘360∘.

Using a calculator, one finds that sin−1(1/10⎯⎯⎯⎯√)=18.4∘sin−1⁡(1/10)=18.4∘ to one decimal place. From the symmetries of sine, we see that the other angles are then 180∘−18.4∘=161.6∘180∘−18.4∘=161.6∘, 180∘+18.4∘=198.4∘180∘+18.4∘=198.4∘ and 360∘−18.4∘=341.6∘360∘−18.4∘=341.6∘, giving us six solutions in total.
