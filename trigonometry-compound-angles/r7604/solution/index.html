Trigonometry: Compound Angles
Review question

Can we solve these simultaneous trigonometric equations?

Solution

  1. Given that x=1+2cosθx=1+2cos⁡θ, prove that x3−3x2+1=2cos3θ−1x3−3x2+1=2cos⁡3θ−1.
Let sinθ=ssin⁡θ=s, and cosθ=ccos⁡θ=c. Looking at the right hand side of the equation we have, using compound angle formulae,
2cos3θ−1=2cos(2θ+θ)−1=2[cos(2θ)c−sin(2θ)s]−1=2[(2c2−1)c−2s2c]−1=2[2c3−c−2(1−c2)c]−1=8c3−6c−1.2cos⁡3θ−1=2cos⁡(2θ+θ)−1=2[cos⁡(2θ)c−sin⁡(2θ)s]−1=2[(2c2−1)c−2s2c]−1=2[2c3−c−2(1−c2)c]−1=8c3−6c−1.
 Looking at the left hand side of the equation, and expanding using the binomial theorem,
x3=(1+2c)3=1+3(2c)+3(2c)2+(2c)3=8c3+12c2+6c+1,x3=(1+2c)3=1+3(2c)+3(2c)2+(2c)3=8c3+12c2+6c+1,
 and
x2=(1+2c)2=4c2+4c+1.x2=(1+2c)2=4c2+4c+1.
 So
x3−3x2+1=(8c3+12c2+6c+1)−3(4c2+4c+1)+1=8c3−6c−1.x3−3x2+1=(8c3+12c2+6c+1)−3(4c2+4c+1)+1=8c3−6c−1.

Hence, x3−3x2+1=2cos3θ−1x3−3x2+1=2cos⁡3θ−1.

Using this result, or otherwise, find each of the three roots of the equation x3−3x2+1=0x3−3x2+1=0, giving your answers correct to 3 places of decimals.

If we use the first part, and take x=1+2cosθx=1+2cos⁡θ, then the equation x3−3x2+1=0x3−3x2+1=0 becomes
⟺⟺⟺2cos3θ−1cos3θ3θθ=0=12=π3+2nπ or−π3+2nπ=π9+2nπ3 or−π9+2nπ32cos⁡3θ−1=0⟺cos⁡3θ=12⟺3θ=π3+2nπ or−π3+2nπ⟺θ=π9+2nπ3 or−π9+2nπ3

for any integer nn.

We can then find xx by working out 1+2cosθ1+2cos⁡θ for these possible values of θθ.

Note that cos(−A)=cosAcos⁡(−A)=cos⁡A for any AA, and that cos(A+2nπ)=cosAcos⁡(A+2nπ)=cos⁡A for any integer nn.

Putting n=0,1,2n=0,1,2 here gives

cosπ9(=cos−π9),cos7π9(=cos11π9)andcos13π9(=cos5π9).cos⁡π9(=cos⁡−π9),cos⁡7π9(=cos⁡11π9)andcos⁡13π9(=cos⁡5π9).

Therefore the three roots (it cannot have more) of x3−3x2+1=0x3−3x2+1=0 are

1+2cosπ9=2.879,1+2cos7π9=−0.532and1+2cos13π9=0.653,1+2cos⁡π9=2.879,1+2cos⁡7π9=−0.532and1+2cos⁡13π9=0.653,
all given to 3 decimal places.

  1. The values of xx and yy satisfy the equations
    3cos2x+2cos2y3sin2x−2sin2y=4,=0.3cos2⁡x+2cos2⁡y=4,3sin⁡2x−2sin⁡2y=0.
     Find the values of cos2xcos⁡2x and cos2ycos⁡2y.

It’s worth trying to get everything in terms of cos2xcos⁡2x and cos2ycos⁡2y first.

We know that cos2x=2cos2x−1cos⁡2x=2cos2⁡x−1; rearranging this gives cos2x=12(1+cos2x)cos2⁡x=12(1+cos⁡2x).

So we can rewrite our first equation as
⟺⟺3cos2x+2cos2y=432(1+cos2x)+22(1+cos2y)=43cos2x+2cos2y=3.3cos2⁡x+2cos2⁡y=4⟺32(1+cos⁡2x)+22(1+cos⁡2y)=4⟺3cos⁡2x+2cos⁡2y=3.
 Now looking at our second equation, we aim to change every sinsin to a coscos. Rearranging and squaring both sides, we have
9sin22x=4sin22y9sin2⁡2x=4sin2⁡2y
 Using the identity cos2θ+sin2θ=1cos2⁡θ+sin2⁡θ=1 gives
⟺⟺9(1−cos22x)=4(1−cos22y)9cos22x−4cos22y=5(3cos2x−2cos2y)(3cos2x+2cos2y)=5.9(1−cos2⁡2x)=4(1−cos2⁡2y)⟺9cos2⁡2x−4cos2⁡2y=5⟺(3cos⁡2x−2cos⁡2y)(3cos⁡2x+2cos⁡2y)=5.

Now substituting in 3cos2x+2cos2y=33cos⁡2x+2cos⁡2y=3 gives

3cos2x−2cos2y=53.3cos⁡2x−2cos⁡2y=53.

We now have two linear simultaneous equations in cos2xcos⁡2x and cos2ycos⁡2y. Adding them to eliminate cos2ycos⁡2y gives

6cos2x=1436cos⁡2x=143
so cos2x=79cos⁡2x=79.

Substituting into either of our simultaneous equations then gives cos2y=13cos⁡2y=13.

Hence find, to the nearest degree, four pairs of corresponding values of xx and yy, all values lying between 0∘0∘ and 360∘360∘.

Using the cos−1cos−1 function on a calculator gives 2x=38.94∘2x=38.94∘ (to four significant figures).

Thus the general solution is 2x=−38.94+360n2x=−38.94+360n, or 2x=38.94+360n2x=38.94+360n.

This gives the four solutions (all to three significant figures) for xx between 0∘0∘ and 360∘360∘:

x=19.5∘,161∘,199∘,341∘.x=19.5∘,161∘,199∘,341∘.

Using a similar method to solve cos2y=13cos⁡2y=13 gives (all to three significant figures):

y=35.3∘,145∘,215∘,325∘.y=35.3∘,145∘,215∘,325∘.

We now need to pair the xx values with the corresponding yy-values.

The first equation is equivalent to 3cos2x+2cos2y=33cos⁡2x+2cos⁡2y=3, and we know the values of cos2xcos⁡2x and cos2ycos⁡2y, so this will not help us further.

However, we squared the second equation (thus introducing extra roots), so if we return to the original here, it should tell us more.

So we have sin2y=32sin2xsin⁡2y=32sin⁡2x.

When x=19.5x=19.5, sin2y=32sin2x=0.943sin⁡2y=32sin⁡2x=0.943, so 2y=70.52y=70.5 or 109109 or 431431 or 469469, giving y=35.3y=35.3, 54.754.7, 215215 or 235235.

Comparing these with our list of possible values of yy from cos2y=13cos⁡2y=13 shows that y=35.3y=35.3 or 215215 in this case.

Likewise, when x=161∘x=161∘, sin2y=32sin2x=−0.943sin⁡2y=32sin⁡2x=−0.943, so 2y=2512y=251 or 289289 or 611611 or 649649, giving y=125y=125 or 145145 or 305305 or 325325.

Thus, comparing with the possible yy values gives y=145y=145 or 325325 in this case.

In the third case, when x=199x=199, we have sin2y=0.943sin⁡2y=0.943 again, so y=35.3y=35.3 or 215215.

Likewise, in the final case, when x=341x=341, we have sin2y=−0.943sin⁡2y=−0.943 again, so y=145y=145 or 325325.

Thus there are in total eight possible pairs of (x,y)(x,y) values: to the nearest degree, these are:

(19∘,35∘),(19∘,215∘),(161∘,145∘),(161∘,325∘),(199∘,35∘),(199∘,215∘),(341∘,145∘),(341∘,325∘).(19∘,35∘),(19∘,215∘),(161∘,145∘),(161∘,325∘),(199∘,35∘),(199∘,215∘),(341∘,145∘),(341∘,325∘).
