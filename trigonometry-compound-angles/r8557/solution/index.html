Trigonometry: Compound Angles
Review question

Can we find the height of a tower using its reflection?
Add to your resource collection

Ref: R8557

Solution

A tower stands on a river bank. From a point on the other bank directly opposite, and at height hh above the water level, the angle of elevation of the top of the tower is αα and the angle of depression of the reflection of the top in the water is ββ. [It is to be assumed that the water is smooth, so that the reflection of any object in the water will appear to be as far below the surface as the tower is above it.] Prove that the height of the top of the tower above the water is

hsin(α+β)cosec(β−α),hsin⁡(α+β)cosec⁡(β−α),
and that the width of the river is
2hcosαcosβcosec(β−α).2hcos⁡αcos⁡βcosec⁡(β−α).

The question wants us to find the height of the tower, tt, and the width of the river, ww. It’s as if someone of height hh is measuring αα and ββ using a theodolite, and is then using these three values to calculate tt and ww. Notice how we deal with the reflection of the tower in our diagram.

We have two right-angled triangles we can work with from the picture. We have

tanα=t−hw,(1)(1)tan⁡α=t−hw,
 from the top triangle, and
tanβ=t+hw,(2)(2)tan⁡β=t+hw,

from the bottom triangle.

We will first find the height of the tower, tt. Rearranging (2)(2) we find that

w=t+htanβ.w=t+htan⁡β.

Substituting this value of ww into equation (1)(1) yields
tanα⟹(t+h)tanα⟹(t+h)sinαcosβ⟹t⟹t=(t−h)tanβt+h=(t−h)tanβ=(t−h)sinβcosα=−h(sinαcosβ+sinβcosα)sinαcosβ−sinβcosα,=hsin(α+β)sin(β−α)=hsin(α+β)cosec(β−α).tan⁡α=(t−h)tan⁡βt+h⟹(t+h)tan⁡α=(t−h)tan⁡β⟹(t+h)sin⁡αcos⁡β=(t−h)sin⁡βcos⁡α⟹t=−h(sin⁡αcos⁡β+sin⁡βcos⁡α)sin⁡αcos⁡β−sin⁡βcos⁡α,⟹t=hsin⁡(α+β)sin⁡(β−α)=hsin⁡(α+β)cosec⁡(β−α).
We have used here that
tanθsin(θ±ϕ)cosecθ=sinθcosθ,=sinθcosϕ±cosθsinϕ, and=1sinθ.tan⁡θ=sin⁡θcos⁡θ,sin⁡(θ±ϕ)=sin⁡θcos⁡ϕ±cos⁡θsin⁡ϕ, andcosec⁡θ=1sin⁡θ.

Now we wish to find the width of the river, ww. We now know what tt is, so we have, using our expression for ww above,

w=t+htanβ=hsin(α+β)cosec(β−α)+htanβ.w=t+htan⁡β=hsin⁡(α+β)cosec⁡(β−α)+htan⁡β.

So we must now rearrange this to check that this agrees with the answer in the question. We have
w=h(sin(α+β)cosβsinβsin(β−α)+cosβsinβ),=hcosβ(sin(α+β)+sin(β−α)sinβsin(β−α)),=hcosβ(2sinβcosαsinβsin(β−α)),=2hcosαcosβcosec(β−α),w=h(sin⁡(α+β)cos⁡βsin⁡βsin⁡(β−α)+cos⁡βsin⁡β),=hcos⁡β(sin⁡(α+β)+sin⁡(β−α)sin⁡βsin⁡(β−α)),=hcos⁡β(2sin⁡βcos⁡αsin⁡βsin⁡(β−α)),=2hcos⁡αcos⁡βcosec⁡(β−α),

as required.

Alternatively we can use the identity

sinθ+sinϕ=2sin(θ+ϕ2)cos(θ−ϕ2).sin⁡θ+sin⁡ϕ=2sin⁡(θ+ϕ2)cos⁡(θ−ϕ2).

Find these two distances (to the nearest foot) when h=15ft.h=15ft., α=24∘α=24∘, and β=31∘β=31∘.

We have
tw=15sin(24+31)sin(31−24)=15sin55sin7=101ft.,=30cos24cos31sin7=193ft.,t=15sin⁡(24+31)sin⁡(31−24)=15sin⁡55sin⁡7=101ft.,w=30cos⁡24cos⁡31sin⁡7=193ft.,

to the nearest foot (using a calculator).

We need to make sure our calculator is in the correct mode (degrees or radians) before making the computation.
