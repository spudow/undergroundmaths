Trigonometry: Compound Angles
Review question

Why is cosθ+cos(θ+2π/3)+cos(θ+4π/3)=0cos⁡θ+cos⁡(θ+2π/3)+cos⁡(θ+4π/3)=0?

Solution

  1. Prove that
    cos(A+B)=cosAcosB−sinAsinB,cos⁡(A+B)=cos⁡Acos⁡B−sin⁡Asin⁡B,
     where the angles AA, BB and A+BA+B are all acute.

We’ll use αα and ββ to represent the angles, and capital letters to represent points on the diagram. So we are trying to prove that

cos(α+β)=cosαcosβ−sinαsinβ.cos⁡(α+β)=cos⁡αcos⁡β−sin⁡αsin⁡β.

The circle drawn has radius 11 (it’s a unit circle). From triangle OCDOCD, we see that OD=cos(α+β)OD=cos⁡(α+β).

From triangle OFCOFC, we know CF=sinβCF=sin⁡β and OF=cosβ.OF=cos⁡β.

From triangle CFGCFG,

sinα=FGFC⟹FG=sinαsinβ.sin⁡α=FGFC⟹FG=sin⁡αsin⁡β.

From triangle OEFOEF,

cosα=OEOF⟹OE=cosαcosβ.cos⁡α=OEOF⟹OE=cos⁡αcos⁡β.

As FGFG is equal in length to DEDE, we have

OE=OD+DE=OD+FG.OE=OD+DE=OD+FG.

Therefore cosαcosβ=cos(α+β)+sinαsinβ,cos⁡αcos⁡β=cos⁡(α+β)+sin⁡αsin⁡β, so cos(α+β)=cosαcosβ−sinαsinβ,cos⁡(α+β)=cos⁡αcos⁡β−sin⁡αsin⁡β, as required.

The restriction to acute angles is actually unnecessary - this works for all angles.

  1. By projecting the sides of an equilateral triangle onto a certain line, prove that
    cosθ+cos(θ+2π3)+cos(θ+4π3)=0cos⁡θ+cos⁡(θ+2π3)+cos⁡(θ+4π3)=0

Let’s start by drawing an equilateral triangle inside a unit circle, at an angle θθ to the horizontal.

We can quickly identify (using parallel lines and angles in triangles) that

∠BAD=θ+2π3and∠CBO (reflex)=θ+4π3.∠BAD=θ+2π3and∠CBO (reflex)=θ+4π3.

Thinking about the unit circle, we can see (by considering the diagrams below) that

If we take ψ=θ+2π3ψ=θ+2π3, we see that

cos(θ+2π3)=AM−→−≡A′B′−→−−,cos⁡(θ+2π3)=AM→≡A′B′→,

and taking ϕ=θ+4π3ϕ=θ+4π3, we have

cos(θ+4π3)=B′O−→−.cos⁡(θ+4π3)=B′O→.

Therefore

cosθ+cos(θ+2π3)+cos(θ+4π3)=OA′−→−+A′B′−→−−+B′O−→−.cos⁡θ+cos⁡(θ+2π3)+cos⁡(θ+4π3)=OA′→+A′B′→+B′O→.

(i.e. we move from OO to A′A′, return to B′B′ and then back to OO).

Therefore the total displacement is zero, and so

cosθ+cos(θ+2π3)+cos(θ+4π3)=0.cos⁡θ+cos⁡(θ+2π3)+cos⁡(θ+4π3)=0.

…and find the value of the expression

sinθ+sin(θ+2π3)+sin(θ+4π3).sin⁡θ+sin⁡(θ+2π3)+sin⁡(θ+4π3).

The sum for sinsin can similarly be found by projecting the sides of an equilateral triangle onto the yy-axis.

We can think again about the unit circle and how we find the sine of different angles:

Let ψ=θ+2π3ψ=θ+2π3 and ϕ=θ+4π3ϕ=θ+4π3. From our diagram above, we get

sinθ+sin(θ+2π3)+sin(θ+4π3)=A′A−→−+MB−→−+BB′−→−=B′M−→−+MB−→−+BB′−→−=0.sin⁡θ+sin⁡(θ+2π3)+sin⁡(θ+4π3)=A′A→+MB→+BB′→=B′M→+MB→+BB′→=0.
