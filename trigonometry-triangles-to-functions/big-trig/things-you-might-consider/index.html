Things you might consider

Which is larger: sin(cosx)sin⁡(cos⁡x) or cos(sinx)cos⁡(sin⁡x)?

Does it depend on xx?

Let’s start by thinking about what we know about the behaviour of sinxsin⁡x and cosx.cos⁡x.

  • Both sinxsin⁡x and cosxcos⁡x have period 2π2π so sin(cosx)sin⁡(cos⁡x) and cos(sinx)cos⁡(sin⁡x) will be periodic. The period of each function can’t be more than 2π2π so we only need to consider what happens for xx in the interval [0,2π].[0,2π].

  • sinxsin⁡x and cosxcos⁡x vary between −1−1 and 1.1. What does this tell us about the minimum and maximum values of sin(cosx)sin⁡(cos⁡x) and cos(sinx)cos⁡(sin⁡x)?

  • For xx in [0,π2][0,π2] and [3π2,2π][3π2,2π] sinxsin⁡x increases and for xx in [π2,3π2][π2,3π2] it decreases. Where does cosxcos⁡x decrease or increase between 00 and 2π2π?

  • We know that cosxcos⁡x is an even function and sinxsin⁡x is an odd function, i.e. cos(−x)=cosxcos⁡(−x)=cos⁡x and sin(−x)=−sinx.sin⁡(−x)=−sin⁡x.

Special points

We’ll consider some multiples of π2π2 to get a sense of how the two functions behave. You may find it helpful to start marking some points on a sketch graph as you read.

  • When x=0,π,2πx=0,π,2π the value of cos(sinx)cos⁡(sin⁡x) is 11, which must be the maximum value of the function. This means the period of cos(sinx)cos⁡(sin⁡x) could be π.π.

  • sin(cos2π)=sin(cos0)=sin1.sin⁡(cos⁡2π)=sin⁡(cos⁡0)=sin⁡1. The value sin1sin⁡1 must be positive, but as sinxsin⁡x does not reach 11 until x=π2x=π2, sin(cos0)<1.sin⁡(cos⁡0)<1. Similarly sin(cosπ)=sin(−1)=−sin1>−1.sin⁡(cos⁡π)=sin⁡(−1)=−sin⁡1>−1.

  • sinπ2=1sin⁡π2=1, so cos(sinπ2)=cos1cos⁡(sin⁡π2)=cos⁡1, which is positive, but less than 1.1. Also, cos(sin3π2)=cos1cos⁡(sin⁡3π2)=cos⁡1 because of the period of cos(sinx).cos⁡(sin⁡x).

  • cosx=0cos⁡x=0 when x=π2x=π2 and x=3π2x=3π2 so sin(cosx)=0sin⁡(cos⁡x)=0 at these points.

This means that cos(sinx)>sin(cosx)cos⁡(sin⁡x)>sin⁡(cos⁡x) at 0,π2,π,3π20,π2,π,3π2 and 2π.2π. The values of cos1cos⁡1 and sin1sin⁡1 seem to be important. Without using a calculator or graphing software, how could you decide which of these values is bigger?

Behaviour between special points

As cosxcos⁡x is negative between π2π2 and 3π23π2 and sinxsin⁡x is negative for xx between −1−1 and 00 the value of the function sin(cosx)sin⁡(cos⁡x) must be negative for xx between π2π2 and 3π2.3π2.

What does the same reasoning tell us about cos(sinx)cos⁡(sin⁡x)?

As cosxcos⁡x is an even function and sinxsin⁡x is odd, cos(sin(−x))=cos(−sinx)=cos(sinx)cos⁡(sin⁡(−x))=cos⁡(−sin⁡x)=cos⁡(sin⁡x) and sin(cos(−x))=sin(cosx)sin⁡(cos⁡(−x))=sin⁡(cos⁡x), so both functions are even. As well as telling us that the yy-axis is a line of symmetry of the graphs of y=sin(cosx)y=sin⁡(cos⁡x) and y=cos(sinx)y=cos⁡(sin⁡x), this also shows that these graphs are symmetric about the line x=π.x=π. (For example, sin(cos(π−x))=sin(cos(x−π)).sin⁡(cos⁡(π−x))=sin⁡(cos⁡(x−π)).) This helps because we are interested in what happens between 00 and 2π.2π.

For xx between 00 and π2π2, sinxsin⁡x increases from 00 to 11 and cosxcos⁡x decreases, so cos(sinx)cos⁡(sin⁡x) decreases from 11 to cos1cos⁡1. On [π2,π][π2,π], sinxsin⁡x decreases from 11 to 00 and cosxcos⁡x decreases from 00 to −1−1. This means that cos(sinx)cos⁡(sin⁡x) increases from cos1cos⁡1 to 11 and therefore has a minimum when x=π2.x=π2. What happens between ππ and 2π2π?

These ideas, can be used to show that cos(sinx)cos⁡(sin⁡x) has a period of ππ and its range is [cos1,1][cos⁡1,1] whereas sin(cosx)sin⁡(cos⁡x) has period 2π2π and its range is [−sin1,sin1].[−sin⁡1,sin⁡1].

For all the points considered, cos(sinx)>sin(cosx)cos⁡(sin⁡x)>sin⁡(cos⁡x) and we now know a lot about the graphs y=cos(sinx)y=cos⁡(sin⁡x) and y=sin(cosx).y=sin⁡(cos⁡x). It seems reasonable to conjecture that cos(sinx)>sin(cosx)cos⁡(sin⁡x)>sin⁡(cos⁡x) for all xx, but to convince ourselves that this is true, we need to show that the graphs do not cross anywhere.

The graphs can’t cross between π2π2 and 3π23π2 because sin(cosx)sin⁡(cos⁡x) is negative here, but what about between 00 and π2π2 or between 3π23π2 and 2π2π?

We want to know whether

cos(sinx)=sin(cosx)cos⁡(sin⁡x)=sin⁡(cos⁡x)
has any solutions for xx in [0,π2].[0,π2]. Now, if cosa=sinbcos⁡a=sin⁡b then a=π2−ba=π2−b, so a+b=π2a+b=π2, or in our case sinx+cosx=π2.sin⁡x+cos⁡x=π2.

The diagram shows part of a circle of radius 11 and an angle xx between 00 and π2.π2. The length of arc APBAPB is π2,π2, but sinxsin⁡x is smaller than the length of arc APAP and cosxcos⁡x is smaller than the length of arc PB.PB.

Therefore

sinx+cosx<π2sin⁡x+cos⁡x<π2
so cos(sinx)=sin(cosx)cos⁡(sin⁡x)=sin⁡(cos⁡x) does not have any solutions in [0,π2][0,π2]. The symmetry of the graphs about x=πx=π, means that there aren’t any solutions in [3π2,2π][3π2,2π] either. Therefore cos(sinx)cos⁡(sin⁡x) is larger than sin(cosx)sin⁡(cos⁡x) for all real x.x.

Another approach is to use the inequality sinθ<θsin⁡θ<θ for positive θ.θ. To see how this inequality arises, consider the diagram below. This shows the chord ABAB of a circle subtending an angle θθ at the centre of the circle, where 0<θ<π.0<θ<π.

If we compare the areas of the triangle OABOAB and the minor sector OAB,OAB, we get 12r2sinθ<12r2θ12r2sin⁡θ<12r2θ and therefore sinθ<θsin⁡θ<θ.

We can combine this inequality with the increasing and decreasing behaviour of sinxsin⁡x and cosxcos⁡x on [0,π2][0,π2] to show that

sin(cosx)<cosxandcos(sinx)>cosxsin⁡(cos⁡x)<cos⁡xandcos⁡(sin⁡x)>cos⁡x

Therefore cosxcos⁡x is always sandwiched between sin(cosx)sin⁡(cos⁡x) and cos(sinx)cos⁡(sin⁡x) for xx between 00 and π2.π2. As above, the symmetry of the graphs completes the proof that cos(sinx)cos⁡(sin⁡x) is larger than sin(cosx)sin⁡(cos⁡x) for all real x.x.
