Vector Geometry
Review question

If ABCDEFGHABCDEFGH is a regular octagon, what do these vectors add to?
Add to your resource collection

Ref: R5992
Printable/supporting materials Fullscreen mode Teacher notes

  • Suggestion
  • Solution

Solution

ABCDEFGHABCDEFGH is a regular octagon. Express in terms of a single vector the sum of the vectors

  1. AC,AH,HGAC,AH,HG,

Vector addition tells us that AH+HG=AGAH+HG=AG.

From the symmetry of the octagon, ACEGACEG must be a square, so the vector AGAG is the same as CECE. The vector sum of ACAC and CECE is AEAE.

Thus we have AC+AH+HG=AEAC+AH+HG=AE.

  1. AB,AC,AF,AH,HGAB,AC,AF,AH,HG.

Using the first part, we can say immediately that AC+AH+HG=AEAC+AH+HG=AE so we are left to sum ABAB, AEAE and AFAF.

But the diagram above shows us that AB+AF=AEAB+AF=AE, since AEAE is the diagonal of the rectangle ABEFABEF.

Thus AB+AC+A+AH+HG=2AEAB+AC+A+AH+HG=2AE.

Given that ||AB||=2,|AB|=2, calculate ||AF|||AF|.

From the symmetry of the octagon, we can see that AFAF is parallel to GHGH. The small triangles on AHAH and FGFG are isoscelese and right-angled, and so

||AF||=2+222=2+22.|AF|=2+222=2+22.
