Vector Geometry
Review question

In a 60km/h60km/h wind, what bearing should this plane take?

Solution

An aeroplane can fly at 300km/h300km/h in still air. If a steady 60km/h60km/h wind is blowing from the direction 030∘030∘, determine

  1. the direction in which the aeroplane must head in order that it actually flies due North,

We can draw a diagram of the velocity vectors for the wind and the aeroplane.

We can now use the sine rule to find the angle αα. We have

sinα60α=sin150∘300=sin−1sin150∘5=5.739...∘.sin⁡α60=sin⁡150∘300α=sin−1⁡sin⁡150∘5=5.739...∘.

The aeroplane needs to take a bearing of 006∘006∘ (to the nearest degree) to counteract the wind and fly due north.

  1. the aeroplane’s resultant velocity.

Using the sine rule

The angle between the velocity vectors of wind and aeroplane is 30∘−α=24.261...∘30∘−α=24.261...∘. Hence we find

sin150∘300x=sin24.261∘x=300sin24.261∘sin150∘.sin⁡150∘300=sin⁡24.261∘xx=300sin⁡24.261∘sin⁡150∘.

This gives a final velocity of the aeroplane of v=246.54...≈247km/hv=246.54...≈247km/h.

Alternatively…

Calculating individual components

The aeroplane’s component of velocity northward is

v1=300cosα.v1=300cos⁡α.

The wind subtracts from this a velocity component of

v2=60cos30∘.v2=60cos⁡30∘.

Hence we obtain v=v1−v2=300cos5.739−60cos30=246.53...≈247km/hv=v1−v2=300cos⁡5.739−60cos⁡30=246.53...≈247km/h, as before.

Would a similar method also work for the first part of the problem?
