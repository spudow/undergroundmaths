Vector Geometry
Review question

Can we show that OAQYOAQY is a parallelogram?

Solution

In the figure the points XX and YY are such that AX=12XBAX=12XB and OY=YXOY=YX, while the point PP is such that OA=3APOA=3AP. The lines YQYQ and PQPQ are parallel to OAOA and OBOB respectively.

Given that OA=aOA=a and OB=bOB=b, express OPOP and OYOY in terms of aa and bb.

Since OA=3APOA=3AP, we know OPOP is 43a43a.

We can see that the vector

OX=OA+AX=a+13(ba)=23a+13b.OX=OA+AX=a+13(ba)=23a+13b.

We also have that the vector

OY=12OX=13a+16b.OY=12OX=13a+16b.

Given that YQ=maYQ=ma and PQ=nbPQ=nb, find the values of mm and nn. Hence show that OAQYOAQY is a parallelogram.

The diagram tells us that OP+PQ=OY+YQOP+PQ=OY+YQ.

Thus

43a+nb=13a+16b+ma.43a+nb=13a+16b+ma.

Since aa and bb are linearly independent (they are not parallel) we can equate coefficients of aa and bb on either side of this equation.

We are effectively saying here that if a+b=a+b,a+b=a+b, then =,=.=,=. Is that always true?

This tells us

43=13+mm=1,43=13+mm=1,
and
n=16.n=16.

Since m=1m=1, we have that ||AO||=||QY|||AO|=|QY|, and we know these two lines are parallel, so therefore OAQYOAQY is a parallelogram.
